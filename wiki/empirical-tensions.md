| **Accuracy vs. abstraction on ARC (textual overestimates, visual underestimates)** — Beger et al. 2025 show that output-grid accuracy is directionally misleading: AI models solve ConceptARC textual tasks via shortcuts ~27-29% of the time (vs. ~5% humans), inflating apparent abstraction; in visual modality, perceptual failures cause models to produce incorrect grids despite forming correct-intended rules (~28% of wrong-grid cases), deflating apparent abstraction. Standard ARC-Prize evaluation (accuracy only) conflates these two error types and cannot distinguish shortcut from genuine concept acquisition. | [[wiki/entities/arc-agi.md]], [[wiki/concepts/abstract-reasoning.md]], [[wiki/papers/beger-conceptarc-multimodal-2025.md]] | Methodological tension; dual-channel evaluation (accuracy + rule quality) is the proposed resolution |
| **Episode inference vs. discrete engram encoding (CH-HNN 2025)** — The canonical engram account holds that each episode is stored in a distinct sparse neuron ensemble allocated via CREB-mediated excitability competition. CH-HNN (Shi et al. 2025) achieves task-agnostic continual learning by routing new episodes through ANN-generated masks derived from prior-knowledge regularities — no per-episode engram allocation is required. This suggests that incremental episodic learning may operate via regularities extracted from prior experience rather than discrete physical memory substrates. | [[wiki/concepts/engrams.md]], [[wiki/concepts/continual-learning.md]], [[wiki/entities/ch-hnn-model.md]], [[wiki/papers/shi-ch-hnn-2025.md]] | Open; engram theory and episode inference may operate at different timescales (acquisition vs. encoding) or be complementary mechanisms for different memory types |
| **HC as domain-general relational processor (relational theory) vs. spatially biased (cognitive map theory)** — Kumaran & Maguire 2005 use a factorial design with two graph-traversal tasks matched on relational complexity, behavioral difficulty, and graph topology (same nodes; edges differ: spatial proximity vs. social acquaintance). HC activates only for the spatial relational task; social relational graph traversal engages mPFC/STS/TPJ with zero medial temporal lobe activation even vs. baseline. The relational theory (Eichenbaum 1993/2004) predicts equal HC engagement for matched relational tasks — directly refuted for large-scale declarative associative graphs. Tension with Dupret 2020 and Bernardi 2020 which show HC participating in non-spatial abstract inference: those tasks involve temporal-sequential structure (pairs ordered in time, contexts alternating across trials) sharing metric properties with navigation. Proposed resolution: HC handles metric or temporal-sequential relational graphs; purely declarative associative graphs (social networks) route to the mPFC/social-brain circuit. **Refined by Garvert et al. 2017:** a *discrete, non-metric* graph with no continuous dimension IS reconstructed in entorhinal cortex — provided it is learned implicitly from experienced transitions (random walks), encoded with an SR/communicability metric. This relocates the dividing line from *metric geometry* to *transition-experience*: HC maps a graph sampled as a transition operator (Garvert) but not one known declaratively without traversal (K&M's social graph). Metric continuity is sufficient, not necessary. **Further refined by Park et al. 2021:** the very domain K&M found gave zero MTL — a *social* hierarchy — **does** recruit an HC+EC 2D map and an EC+mPFC grid code when it is 2D-metric-embeddable (orderable ranks), reconstructed from experienced binary comparisons, and inferentially traversed for a decision. So "social" per se never mattered: the operative variable is **embeddability + inferential use**, and K&M's negative is specifically about a *declaratively-known, non-inferentially-used* graph. Park is the active/explicit counterpart to Garvert's passive/implicit case. **Further refined by Zheng et al. 2024** (reanalysis of Garvert's dataset): a *lifetime-acquired, explicit, taxonomic-semantic* structure (object category space) is **also** mapped in HC (posterior HC), *passively*, alongside the transition map (anterior EC) — as **parallel, anatomically separable** maps, not one conjunctive map. This narrows K&M's negative once more: the disqualifier is **non-metric / non-embeddable structure specifically**, not declarative-ness, explicitness, recency, or lack of traversal — taxonomic similarity is embeddable and gets an HC map despite being explicit and lifetime-old. It also adds a *parallel-maps* design point: multiple System-1 structures over one entity set coexist separably and require a selection stage, rather than fusing. | [[wiki/entities/hippocampal-entorhinal-system.md]], [[wiki/papers/kumaran-maguire-2005-hippocampus.md]], [[wiki/papers/garvert-abstract-relational-map-2017.md]], [[wiki/papers/park-social-hierarchy-grid-code-2021.md]], [[wiki/papers/zheng-parallel-cognitive-maps-2024.md]], [[wiki/papers/inferential-reasoning-dupret-2020.md]], [[wiki/papers/geometry-abstraction-bernardi-2020.md]] | Open — resolved for declaratively-known non-embeddable associative graphs (HC not engaged) and for embeddable graphs whether transition-sampled (Garvert), actively-inferred (Park), or taxonomic-semantic (Zheng) (HC engaged); routing key refined to **embeddability alone** (metric geometry OR SR-encodable transitions OR taxonomic-similarity space); HC holds multiple embeddable structures as parallel separable maps; still untested for genuinely non-embeddable symbolic structure |
| **Goldman-Rakic maintenance-memory theory vs. PFdl attentional dominance (Lebedev 2004)** — Goldman-Rakic (1987) proposed PFdl functions primarily in short-term memory maintenance; Lebedev et al. 2004 directly dissociated spatial attention from spatial memory in PFdl single neurons and found 61% of spatially tuned cells encode the *attended* location vs. 16% the *remembered* one, with the attended representation growing stronger as the trigger approached. Proposed resolution: PFdl contributes to maintenance memory but this is not its primary function; attentional selection is the dominant computation. Memory deficits after PFC lesions may largely reflect attentional failures rather than loss of a dedicated memory buffer. Dorsomedial PFdl (~8%/9% balanced) and hybrid cells (~23%) preserve a partial memory-encoding role. | [[wiki/entities/prefrontal-cortex.md]], [[wiki/concepts/working-memory.md]], [[wiki/papers/lebedev-pfdl-attention-memory-2004.md]] | Settled framing adopted; maintenance-memory remains a partial account in dorsomedial PFdl and hybrid cells |
| **Solvers demonstrably do research math (IMO gold, Erdős solves) vs. wiki's "latent structure inference is the unsolved bottleneck (<5% frontier)"** — The 2026 math survey documents the LLM-generator + external-verifier stack producing IMO-2025 gold, MiniF2F 25%→93%, and four Lean-verified Erdős solves, which superficially refutes the wiki's claim that latent graph discovery is unsolved. Proposed resolution (not a true contradiction): the solver stack *externalizes* the graph (mathlib/DSL/program space), the verifier (Lean kernel/fitness), and the vocabulary (given libraries) — it never learns an internal latent graph. It therefore wins exactly where infrastructure exists and ceilings exactly where vocabulary is latent (FrontierMath Tier-4 single-digit; Tao's "discovery modulo expertise"), which is the same unknown-vocabulary source (2) the wiki isolates. The two literatures describe opposite faces of one wall. | [[wiki/queries/brain-inspired-vs-solver-approach.md]], [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]], [[wiki/entities/frontiermath-benchmark.md]], [[wiki/papers/math-reasoning-survey-2026.md]] | Resolved as complementary (externalized vs. internalized LGD), not contradictory; the shared frontier is vocabulary co-discovery |
| **Verified ARC-AGI-2 frontier (24% open Kaggle / 54% audited harness) vs. closed-lab self-reports (≤85%, GPT-5.5)** — llm-stats (Jul 2026) lists closed frontier models at 58–85% on ARC-AGI-2 (GPT-5.5 85.0%, Gemini 3.1 Pro 77.1%, Claude Opus 4.6 68.8%), but these are self-reported on closed-source models with no reproducible harness or independent audit. The highest ARC-Prize-verified results are 24% (open Kaggle, cost-capped $0.20/task) and 54% (audited Gemini 3 Pro refinement harness, ~$31/task). Burden of proof is on the labs: scores cannot be taken at face value until model + data are open. ARC Prize also notes high closed-model scores may partly reflect knowledge coverage on the shared public/private task distribution. | [[wiki/entities/arc-agi.md]], [[wiki/papers/arc-prize-2025-technical-report.md]] | Open — verified and self-reported frontiers diverge ~30 pts; unverifiable until closed models are released |
| **Core systems all-or-none unitary (Spelke 2022) vs. dissociable sub-principles (Revencu & Csibra 2023)** — Spelke treats core systems as unitary: an entity failing one object principle (e.g. cohesion) loses all objecthood, and she uses this all-or-none character to argue the composite SOCIAL AGENT concept is *not* a core system (agency/intentionality dissociate). Revencu & Csibra counter with Stahl & Feigenson (2015): infants selectively explore an object by *which* principle it violated (bang a solidity-violator, drop a gravity-violator) rather than suspending all objecthood; continuity dissociates from cohesion (teleportation) and action-on-contact is violable (remote controls) without demoting objecthood. If even the object system is non-unitary, non-unity cannot be used to rule out a single social-agent system. **Design reading:** a core prior is a bundle of separately-maskable sub-constraints, not a monolithic binary gate — the objectness prior (Gap #9) should be implemented as dissociable sub-principles. | [[wiki/concepts/core-knowledge.md]], [[wiki/papers/revencu-csibra-what-babies-know-2023.md]], [[wiki/papers/spelke-kinzler-core-knowledge-2007.md]] | Open — evidence favors dissociable sub-principles; bears on how discrete-entity priors are structured architecturally |
| **Sparse localist coding (biological SDR) vs. dense polysemantic superposition (LLM factual recall)** — Biological engrams and neocortical SDRs achieve high capacity via *sparse* (2–6% DG), near-orthogonal coding with a closed-form interference guarantee (Ahmad & Hawkins 2016). Nanda et al. 2023 show Pythia 2.8B stores factual knowledge via *dense* polysemantic superposition instead: ablating individual MLP neurons shows near-zero cross-fact correlation (maximal distribution, not sparse localist allocation), and only one weakly interpretable unit (a "baseball neuron") emerged. Whether biological and artificial memory converge on sparse vs. dense solutions may depend on whether content must support fast one-shot writes (biological HC) vs. only slow batch consolidation (LLM pretraining) — sparsity may be required specifically for one-shot Hebbian binding, not for memorization per se. | [[wiki/concepts/sparse-distributed-representations.md]], [[wiki/concepts/engrams.md]], [[wiki/papers/fact-finding-factual-recall-nanda-2023.md]] | Open — may reflect different write-timescale constraints rather than a true contradiction |
| **Spontaneous world model as evidence for the navigation frame (Nanda et al. 2023) vs. presence ≠ exclusivity (MoveFirst)** — OthelloGPT spontaneously builds a board-state graph from pure next-move SSL and the geometry is *causally used* (vector-arithmetic steering changes predictions) — the strongest **usage test** in the central-framing audit (in silico), supporting sub-claim C's "the map is a natural attractor, not an imposed metaphor." *But* the same paper's **MoveFirst / multiple-circuits** result shows that in end-games the model computes legal moves via simpler non-navigational circuits *before/without* the full board state — a correct, causally-used world model coexists with shortcut circuits that bypass it whenever they suffice. This is the ML mirror of Chen's neuroscience deflation: a structured representation being *present and causal* still does not license "the computation *is* navigation." Proposed framing: usage tests must show the map is **load-bearing when a cheaper shortcut is available** (the ML analog of Qu's incongruent-trial dissociation), not merely present, linear, and steerable. **Reinforced + a second caveat (Tehenan et al. 2025):** a *text-only* general-purpose LLM (LLaMA-3.2) spontaneously builds a linear R³ spatial world model — relations compose by vector addition, and steering the subspace changes stated relations (74.3%) — strengthening the "natural attractor" claim by removing Othello's game-specificity *and* full-observability (language only partially observes space). But its 74.3% is *strongly asymmetric* (`behind` 5% vs. `above` 100%), so a usage test can **pass in aggregate yet fail per-axis** — "presence ≠ uniform usability," a second deflationary refinement beside MoveFirst's "presence ≠ exclusivity." Also nuances the wiki's "LLMs are shallow world models" claim: text-only training yields a genuine, causally-used *static* spatial model; only *dynamics* (transition function) stay untested/shallow. **Third emergence source + a training-dynamics link and a new caveat (Ivanitskiy et al. 2023):** a maze-solving GPT compresses the entire maze *connectivity graph* (LGD's own object) into a single token, decodable at layer 2, with adjacency heads respecting topology in-context — and representation emergence co-occurs with **grokking-like generalization jumps** (the one source linking world-model formation to a generalization signature). But it is the *weakest* on usage (correlational DLA only, no ablations) and adds a third deflationary caveat beside MoveFirst and steering-asymmetry: **"presence ≠ necessity"** — the smaller `hallway` model solves its task *without* forming a clear linear map, and both models wall-jump (shortcut co-presence). | [[wiki/papers/emergent-linear-world-models-nanda-2023.md]], [[wiki/papers/linear-spatial-world-models-tehenan-2025.md]], [[wiki/papers/structured-maze-transformers-2023.md]], [[wiki/concepts/world-models.md]], [[wiki/concepts/shortcut-reasoning.md]], [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]], [[wiki/queries/central-framing-epistemic-audit.md]] | Open — spontaneous emergence now triangulated across three ML substrates (game / text-R³ / maze-graph) and causally used in two of three; MoveFirst + steering-asymmetry + hallway-no-map add "presence ≠ exclusivity / uniform usability / necessity" caveats matching Chen's deflation from the ML side |
| **Grid-code ubiquity/detection deflation (Chen et al. 2022) vs. abstract grid signal as strong evidence for the navigation frame** — The wiki treats abstract-domain hexagonal fMRI signatures (Constantinescu 2016) as strong, model-independent evidence that a metric grid code generalizes to abstract reasoning (audit sub-claim A). Chen et al. argue grid-like responses may be a *universal* cortical byproduct (single-unit grids in rat S1/V2; predicted in auditory + frontal cortex) emerging generically from SR eigenvectors *or* RNN attractor dynamics — which cuts both ways: (a) grid detection is an "arbitrary threshold phenomenon" prone to false positives (spatial autocorrelation), human abstract grid codes are **BOLD-only, never single-unit-confirmed**, and detectable only under **contrived ≈2D task designs** ("lack of structure in tasks brings a difficulty of detecting generalized grid codes"); (b) if grids are ubiquitous and generic, their presence during an abstract task is **weak evidence** that reasoning *is* navigation — they would appear during any structured recurrent computation. Tempers sub-claim A and deflates sub-claim C. Proposed framing: abstract grid signatures are **necessary-but-not-diagnostic**; the frame needs a positive **usage test** (does perturbing/reading grid geometry change inference behavior?), not mere co-occurrence. **Partial resolution (Qu et al. 2026):** grid-code strength *mediates* the age→inference gain and *moderates the congruency effect* — it predicts improvement specifically on incongruent trials that provably require 2D structure, not on heuristic-solvable congruent trials. This is a **behavioral-necessity gradient** (grid presence tracks the trials that need it), supplying most of the demanded usage test short of causal perturbation, and partly re-inflating the abstract grid signal's evidential weight — though still BOLD-only on an orderable-2D design (Chen's single-unit + contrived-task caveats persist). **Second usage line (Park et al. 2021):** grid alignment *modulates decision value* — growth-potential coding in mPFC/TPJ is stronger for grid-aligned than misaligned inferred trajectories, and EC grid strength correlates with TPJ value coding — i.e. the grid frame is *coupled to the choice computation*, a value-usage link complementary to Qu's congruency gradient (both correlational, short of perturbation). Park's design, however, *engineered equidistant ranks* to make the grid measurable — the sharpest instance of Chen's contrived-task caveat. | [[wiki/entities/grid-cells.md]], [[wiki/papers/grid-like-all-perception-chen-2022.md]], [[wiki/papers/gridlikecode-constantinescu-2016.md]], [[wiki/papers/qu-grid-code-development-intelligence-2026.md]], [[wiki/papers/park-social-hierarchy-grid-code-2021.md]], [[wiki/queries/central-framing-epistemic-audit.md]] | Open — deflationary caveat adopted; usage evidence (Qu congruency gradient + Park value-coupling) partially restores the abstract grid signal; single-unit + non-contrived-task + causal-perturbation confirmation still pending |
| **Program induction / abduction as a non-navigational reduction of ARC (Johnson et al. 2021) vs. "reasoning ≅ navigation over a latent graph" (audit sub-claim C)** — The first human ARC study (83.8% vs. 21%/57.5% program-synthesis) explains the wiki's own target benchmark with *no* navigation vocabulary: **abduction over an unbounded conceptual store** (rules invented per task, not selected from a fixed primitive set / pre-given meta-graph), **flexible task-dependent object parsing**, and **natural language as the hypothesis-generation scaffold** (description length → difficulty, b=−0.17). This is the Axis-2 rival on the discrete-symbolic slice where C was already a bet. **Not a clean contradiction:** it is an algorithmic-level account with no neural claim (cannot contradict the grid-code *substrate*), and program search is *foldable* into hypothesis-graph navigation. Proposed framing: the two are not rivals *everywhere* — metric/transition-sampled structure → grid/map code (Axes 1/3/4, strong); discrete non-embeddable symbolic inference → program induction (Axis 2). The *strong* C ("all reasoning is navigation") breaks; the *modular* reading (navigation = the metric-slice substrate, one module beside a program-induction System 2) survives. Folding program induction into LGD is a modeling choice, not a finding. | [[wiki/papers/johnson-human-program-induction-arc-2021.md]], [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]], [[wiki/concepts/abstract-reasoning.md]], [[wiki/queries/central-framing-epistemic-audit.md]], [[wiki/entities/arc-agi.md]] | Open — rival reduction owns the discrete-symbolic slice natively; demotes strong C to "one contestable frame"; **Goodman 2024 (#6) and Butz 2016 (#7) now ingested** — reframes the contest as cross-Marr-level across three frames (see three-reductions row below); Butz de-privileges navigation as *substrate*, closing Axis 2 |
| **Topological (graph-connectivity) vs. Euclidean (metric) coding within the spatial system (Epstein et al. 2017)** — During real-world navigation the hippocampal formation uses *two* codes whose relationship is unresolved: **EC tracks Euclidean distance-to-goal** (straight-line, barrier-independent) while **HC tracks graph-topological quantities** — path distance plus degree centrality (# connected streets) and closeness centrality (global connectivity) (Howard/Javadi Soho studies; Wu & Foster replay preserves track topology). Epstein et al.: "unclear how this topological coding of space relates to a possible Euclidean spatial code." Significance for the central-framing audit: the brain holds a **non-metric graph code even for physical space** — a middle term between the metric grid code (audit's strong slice) and genuinely non-embeddable symbolic structure (audit's open slice). The audit's "metric-strong / symbolic-bet" dichotomy under-registers this graph-topological code, which may be the bridge from embeddable to non-embeddable structure (a topological/connectivity code needs no metric embedding). Bears on whether the grid substrate extends to declarative graphs via a topological rather than Euclidean route. | [[wiki/entities/hippocampal-entorhinal-system.md]], [[wiki/papers/epstein-cognitive-map-humans-2017.md]], [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]], [[wiki/queries/central-framing-epistemic-audit.md]] | Open — two coexisting spatial codes (metric EC + topological HC) whose integration is uncharacterized; the topological code is an under-explored candidate bridge to non-embeddable structure |
| **Three "one-problem" reductions collide: PLoT/program induction (Goodman 2024) vs. free-energy/attractor (Butz 2016) vs. LGD/navigation (audit sub-claim C)** — PLoT claims the *same* universality LGD claims (all cognition = conditional inference `query` over compositional stochastic programs), backed by a **universality theorem** (stochastic λ-calculus = any computable distribution) where LGD's "reasoning ≅ navigation" is a *framing bet*. The two are **mutually foldable**: navigation-over-a-graph is literally one stochastic program (PLoT subsumes LGD), while folding PLoT into LGD (nodes=programs, edges=program-moves) is the tautological frame-preserving absorption the audit flags — so *neither* foldability establishes *the* reduction. **Not a clean contradiction:** PLoT is computational-level (Marr-1) with no neural claim, so it cannot falsify the navigation *substrate*, only de-privilege navigation as *the* reduction; and PLoT has its own tractability wall (inference intractability) mirroring LGD's. **Butz 2016 adds the third frame — and attacks the substrate PLoT could not:** the **free-energy reduction** (concepts = distributed free-energy-minimum attractors, compositions = merged attractors, episodes = attractor successions) is pitched at Marr's *implementation* level, so it contests the navigation **substrate** directly, demoting spatial/navigational coding to **1 of 3 primitive encoding types** (temporal/spatial/top-down) sited in **parietal, not entorhinal, cortex**. It too is mutually foldable (event schemata ≈ edges, episodes ≈ paths), and is an *unimplemented sketch* with the same global-attractor intractability wall → it **de-privileges, does not falsify**. **Proposed framing:** the honest synthesis is a **three-frame cross-Marr stack** — PLoT = *what* is computed; navigation-map and free-energy-attractor = two candidate *hows* (themselves possibly mutually foldable) — so navigation is **one of ≥3 contestable lenses at both Marr levels**, never the established reduction. The decisive open test is the **level-connection** question (Axis-4): is the inference realized *on* an emergent map, on non-navigational sampling dynamics, or on free-energy-attractor relaxation? | [[wiki/concepts/probabilistic-language-of-thought.md]], [[wiki/concepts/predictive-coding.md]], [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]], [[wiki/papers/goodman-plot-probabilistic-programs-2024.md]], [[wiki/papers/butz-unified-subsymbolic-cognition-2016.md]], [[wiki/queries/central-framing-epistemic-audit.md]] | Open — reframed as cross-Marr-level complementarity across three frames, not same-level contradiction; all three Axis-2 rivals now ingested (Johnson #5, Goodman #6, Butz #7); Axis 2 closed — adjudication of the *hows* awaits the level-connection test |
