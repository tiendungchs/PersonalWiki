# The cognitive map in humans: spatial navigation and beyond

**Citation:** Epstein RA, Patai EZ, Julian JB, Spiers HJ (2017). *Nat. Neurosci.* 20(11), 1504–1513. doi:10.1038/nn.4656

The **measured-review skeptic** of the central-framing audit (Axis-3 #9) — the complement to Chen 2022's devil's advocate. A framing source, not new data: its primary evidence (Constantinescu, Doeller, Tavares, Howard, Javadi) is already ingested. Its value is the honest statement of *how far the map metaphor legitimately extends* — and its decomposition of navigation into three pillars, only one of which the audit has actually scored.

## Key computational insights

- **Cognitive-map navigation has three separable pillars, proposed to generalize to abstract domains:** (i) **spatial coding** — HC/EC map-like + grid codes preserving distance; (ii) **landmark anchoring** — fixing the map's orientation/position to stable environmental references (boundaries, geometry, objects), computed largely in RSC/PPA/OPA; (iii) **route planning** — HC retrieves path options (traces future trajectories, codes graph topology), PFC evaluates them. The audit and wiki have scored the frame almost entirely on pillar (i).
- **The anchoring pillar has essentially no demonstrated abstract analog** — "there have been no reports of cells that fire to the 'boundary' of a concept or a social milieu." Anchoring (how you localize/orient on a map, fix it to invariants) is the least-generalized part of the frame. A new axis of incompleteness for "reasoning ≅ navigation," orthogonal to metric-vs-symbolic.
- **Context retrieval vs. orientation** — the proposed domain-general principle: *context retrieval* = recover the map appropriate to the current environment (PPA→HC; abstract: bring up the right social/semantic space); *orientation* = determine one's coordinates & heading on it (RSC; abstract: align to salient dimensions like affiliation/hierarchy or semantic prototypes). Two operations any map-navigation reasoner needs, currently conflated in the wiki's configurator story.
- **Topological vs. Euclidean coding, unresolved:** EC tracks Euclidean distance-to-goal; HC tracks *graph-topological* quantities — path distance plus degree centrality (# connected streets) and closeness centrality (global connectivity) (Javadi/Howard). The authors: "unclear how this topological coding of space relates to a possible Euclidean spatial code." The brain has a non-metric graph code even for physical space.
- **RSC anchoring mechanism:** bidirectional cells code heading in a *local* geometric frame; classical HD cells code *global* heading — RSC mediates local↔global reference-frame transformation (the alignment operation an "orientation" stage requires).
- **Route planning = imagining future state sequences** (HC preplay at choice points; lateral PFC breadth-first-search cost; dmPFC hierarchical planning by grouping regions; PPC allocentric→egocentric) — the substrate the authors extend to episodic future thought and prospective reasoning.

## Limitations

- A 2017 review: no new primary data; the abstract-domain claims rest on then-nascent studies the authors themselves flag as promising-but-open.
- **The champions' own hedge:** "it may not turn out to be the case that all cognitive maps are supported by the same mechanistic rules" — map-for-everything is stated as a hypothesis, from its strongest advocates. Directly supports demoting audit sub-claim C from finding to frame.
