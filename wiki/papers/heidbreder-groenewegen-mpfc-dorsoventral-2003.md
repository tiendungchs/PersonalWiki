# Rat mPFC: Dorso-Ventral Distinction (Heidbreder & Groenewegen 2003)

Heidbreder CA, Groenewegen HJ. *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews* 27 (2003) 555–579. Foundational review arguing the rat medial PFC splits into a **dorsal** (FR2 + dorsal anterior cingulate ACd + dorsal prelimbic PLd) and a **ventral** (ventral prelimbic PLv + infralimbic IL + medial orbital MO) component, convergent across four evidence types: behavior, connectivity, neurochemistry, immediate-early genes.

## Key computational insights

- **Two orthogonal functional roles (an axis *orthogonal* to the rostro-caudal rule hierarchy):**
  - **Dorsal mPFC** = *temporal patterning of behavioral sequences*, response-set maintenance, shifting away from previously-rewarded locations (response perseveration), attention, sensorimotor action planning.
  - **Ventral mPFC** = *flexible shifting to new strategies/rules* in spatial and visual-cued discrimination (set-shifting, reversal, cross-modal shift), plus integration of internal physiological state with salient cues under threat/aversion. Ventral is **not** needed to *acquire* a rule — it is needed to *switch* rules.
- **PL vs IL = goal-directed vs habit.** Prelimbic mediates voluntary, goal-directed responding; infralimbic mediates the overtraining-driven transition to behavioral autonomy (habit) — a clean anatomical model-based/model-free (System 2 / System 1) dissociation. IL also consolidates *long-term* fear-extinction memory (inhibitory rule).
- **HC→mPFC is virtually unidirectional.** Ventral subiculum/CA1 → IL/PLv is dense; almost no direct mPFC→HC return (indirect via entorhinal or via nucleus reuniens). Anatomical grounding for the "ventral-HC provides context that selects the mPFC schema" channel.
- **Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical re-entrant loops with a ventral→dorsal feed-forward:** ventral areas project to accumbens **shell** → ventral pallido-thalamo → more **dorsal** cortices; dorsal areas project to accumbens **core**/dorsomedial caudate-putamen. Deep vs superficial layers target striatal patch vs matrix; deep ventral layers have the strongest patch (→ midbrain DA) input.
- **Dorso-ventral neuromodulatory gradient** (see [[wiki/concepts/neuromodulation.md]]): ventral PL/IL densely DA-innervated from VTA/A10 (deep layers, low DAT → high extracellular DA); superficial ACC DA from SNc/A9 (high DAT → high uptake, low extracellular DA). ACh into PL/IL (not ACC) supports spatial WM (scopolamine impairs, oxotremorine rescues). IL↔dorsal raphe; prelimbic 5-HT outflow tracks impulsivity. Ventral mPFC provides the stronger feedback to brainstem monoaminergic nuclei and to VTA.
- **Homology:** rat PL ≈ Brodmann area 32, IL ≈ area 25, ACC ≈ area 24 (proisocortical/archicortical origin); IL is the least cytoarchitecturally differentiated (agranular).

## Limitations

Rat-only; much of the review concerns autonomic/affective/pharmacology (blood pressure, drug sensitization, fear extinction) not directly about abstract reasoning. Dorso-ventral boundaries are *gradients* that cut across cytoarchitectonic borders, not sharp modules. Cross-species mapping of rodent PL/IL to primate/human mPFC subdivisions is contested.
