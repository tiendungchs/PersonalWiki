# Prefrontal atrophy, disrupted NREM slow waves, and impaired hippocampal-dependent memory in aging

Mander, Rao, Lu, Saletin, Lindquist, Ancoli-Israel, Jagust & Walker. *Nature Neuroscience* 16(3): 357–364 (2013). doi: 10.1038/nn.3324

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **mPFC is the dominant source generator of NREM slow waves (SWA: 0.8–4.6 Hz).** EEG topography confirms peak SWA over prefrontal derivations in both young and older adults; mPFC grey matter volume predicts global SWA (r = 0.89, p < 0.001). Other atrophy-prone regions (hippocampus, temporal lobe, precuneus) do not mediate age effects on SWA — the relationship is regionally specific to mPFC.
- **Three-way mediation chain:** age → mPFC grey matter atrophy → ↓SWA → ↓overnight episodic memory retention. Sobel mediation for mPFC→SWA: p = 0.005; SWA→memory: p < 0.001. Adding SWA to the model renders both age (p = 0.249) and mPFC grey matter (p = 0.357) non-significant — SWA fully accounts for both.
- **HC-independence as the functional outcome of successful consolidation.** SWA strength negatively predicts post-sleep hippocampal retrieval activation (r ≈ −0.7 to −0.8); reduced SWA produces persistent HC dependence and reduced HC-vmPFC functional connectivity during retrieval — the two-step neural signature of failed hippocampal-neocortical memory transformation.
- **SWA is distinct from SWRs.** SWA (0.8–4.6 Hz slow oscillations) is the oscillatory context within which SWRs are nested; sleep spindle density, total sleep time, sleep efficiency, and percent SWS do not independently predict memory change once SWA is controlled — isolating SWA as the critical variable.
- Young adults show a significant sleep-vs-wake memory retention benefit; older adults do not — confirming that sleep-specific mPFC-generated SWA, not merely elapsed time, drives the consolidation advantage.

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional design; mediation is statistical not causal — unmeasured factors co-varying with age, mPFC atrophy, and SWA cannot be excluded.
- Small sample (n = 15 older, n = 18 young adults); hippocampal fMRI ROI focused on a single a priori coordinate.
- Episodic word-pair task; generalization to semantic, procedural, or abstract relational schema consolidation is inferred, not directly tested.
