# Matsuzaka et al. 2012 — pmPFC and Strategic Tactic Selection

Matsuzaka Y, Akiyama T, Tanji J, Mushiake H. *PNAS* 109(12):4633–4638. doi:10.1073/pnas.1119971109

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Tactic selection ≠ action selection ≠ conflict monitoring.** pmPFC (~area 8b, rostral to pre-SMA) encodes *which response protocol to use* (reach-toward vs. reach-away), not which specific action. ACC had only 5/260 concordance-selective neurons — conflict monitoring and tactic selection are distinct operations in distinct areas.
- **Demand-responsive representation.** 51% of pmPFC neurons are concordance-selective in the mixed-tactic condition; the population nearly disappears under single-tactic conditions (concordant-only or discordant-only), even though actions continue to vary. The representation is conditionally engaged — only when ≥2 tactics must be simultaneously available.
- **Predictive, not reactive.** Neuronal ensemble onset of tactic discrimination: ~125–145ms before the animal responds (vs. reaction times of 247–313ms). pmPFC signals the tactic before the motor system acts.
- **Non-selective neurons provide tonic readiness.** ~49% of task-related pmPFC neurons fire equally for both tactics — they provide a general-purpose excitatory drive enabling rapid tactic expression. Selective neurons (51%) discriminate which tactic is required.
- **Controlled→automatic transition.** After single-tactic training, pmPFC disengages; reaction times decrease (faster automatic execution). Returning to mixed conditions re-recruits the pmPFC and re-lengthens reaction times. Consistent with supervisory control being bypassed once behavior is compiled into a habit (cortico-BG loop).

## Limitations

- Single task (visually-cued two-choice reach); generalization to abstract tactic domains unstested.
- No causal intervention (electrical stimulation) — lesion or pharmacological studies needed to confirm necessity.
- Only two animals (*Macaca fuscata*); recordings are correlational.
