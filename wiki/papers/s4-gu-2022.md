# Efficiently Modeling Long Sequences with Structured State Spaces

Gu, Goel & Ré — Stanford University, ICLR 2022.

---

## Citation

Albert Gu, Karan Goel, Christopher Ré. "Efficiently Modeling Long Sequences with Structured State Spaces." ICLR 2022.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **NPLR parameterization**: HiPPO matrix A decomposed as Normal + Low-Rank (Λ − PQ*); Woodbury identity reduces the SSM kernel to a Cauchy kernel computation, achieving O(N+L) from O(N²L)
- **HiPPO initialization is critical**: switching A from random Gaussian to HiPPO improves sMNIST from 60% → 98%; ablation confirms the structure of A, not the NPLR algorithm, drives performance
- **Dual representation**: convolutional mode (parallel FFT-based training) ↔ recurrent mode (O(N) per-step inference); switching mode enables 60× faster autoregressive generation than Transformers
- **Continuous-time adaptivity**: step size Δ can be changed at test time, giving free sampling-rate adaptation without retraining
- **SoTA on LRA**: 86.09% average across 6 tasks (vs. <60% for all Transformer variants); first model to solve Path-X (length 16,384) at 96.35%

---

## Limitations

- Linear time-invariant: fixed A, B, C matrices cannot adapt per-token (Mamba addresses this with selective gating, at the cost of hierarchical reasoning performance)
- Evaluated on sequence tasks; not yet demonstrated at LLM scale
- Numerical instabilities from eigenvalues on the right half-plane addressed post-hoc by S4D variant (Λ − PP* rather than Λ − PQ*)
