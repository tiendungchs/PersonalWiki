# Dynamic Network Connectivity: A New Form of Neuroplasticity — Arnsten et al. 2010

**Citation:** Arnsten AFT, Paspalas CD, Gamo NJ, Yang Y, Wang M. *Trends Cogn Sci.* 2010 Aug;14(8):365–375. PMID: 20554470.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **DNC operates in seconds**: molecular signaling events in PFC dendritic spines open/close HCN and KCNQ K⁺ channels to rapidly and reversibly shunt or amplify network inputs — a timescale 3–4 orders of magnitude faster than LTP/LTD (hours).
- **cAMP is the central hub**: elevated cAMP opens HCN (directly) and KCNQ (via PKA) channels → shunts inputs → weakens network. Inhibiting cAMP closes HCN/KCNQ and opens TRPC depolarizing channels → strengthens network. All major neuromodulatory axes converge on cAMP.
- **NE α2A-AR = strengthening gate**: Gi-coupled α2A-adrenergic receptors on PFC spines inhibit cAMP → close HCN channels → strengthen preferred-direction firing; co-localized with HCN channels at spine head and neck. Guanfacine (α2A agonist) improves WM under distraction.
- **DA D1R inverted-U in PFC**: moderate D1R stimulation elevates cAMP in nonpreferred-direction spines → sculpts network (noise ↓); high D1R (stress) → cAMP surge → global network collapse.
- **Stress = DNC weaponized**: acute stress triggers massive NE + DA release → cAMP surge → HCN/KCNQ open → PFC offline; simultaneously amygdala and striatum strengthen. Molecular implementation of the integration→segregation switch.
- **WM capacity limit = epilepsy prevention**: negative feedback (Ca²⁺ → SK channels; cAMP → HCN/KCNQ) that prevents seizures in recurrent PFC circuits also limits WM storage duration to ~10–30 sec; HC engagement required beyond this horizon.
- **Long thin spines are the DNC substrate**: DNC synapses occupy long, pedunculated spines with narrow necks (high electrical resistance → effective shunting); distinct from mushroom spines of LTP/LTD which enlarge structurally.
- **ACh strengthens via two routes**: α7 nicotinic receptors on spines potentiate NMDA inputs directly; muscarinic M1 receptors close KCNQ channels ("M-current") → depolarize.

## Limitations

- Mechanistic detail is from dorsolateral PFC of monkeys; rodent mPFC generalizations tentative.
- DNC modulatory effects are only visible in cognitively engaged (in vivo, delay-period active) circuits; in vitro results may not transfer.
- Chronic stress → architectural changes (spine retraction) are a downstream consequence; DNC per se is rapid and reversible — the two timescales are conflated in some analyses.
