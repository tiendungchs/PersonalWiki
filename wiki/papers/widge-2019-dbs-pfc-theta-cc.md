# DBS of the Internal Capsule Enhances Human Cognitive Control and PFC Function

Widge, Zorowitz, Basu, Paulk, Cash, Eskandar, Deckersbach, Miller & Dougherty — *Nature Communications* 2019. PMID 30948727.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **VCVS DBS causally enhances CC:** 14 psychiatric patients (MDD/OCD) with VCVS DBS implants performed the Multi-Source Interference Task (MSIT); DBS ON reduced RT by 34 ms (p<1e–17); error rate unchanged; effect specific to conflict task (not motor speed, not impulsive responding).
- **Mechanism is corticostriatal → PFC theta:** DBS retrograde-activates corticofugal fibers passing through the ventral internal capsule/ventral striatum → increases non-phase-locked theta (4–8 Hz) power in VLPFC (anterior IFG) and mPFC; frequency-specific (alpha/beta unchanged); effect specific to other bands does not survive FDR correction.
- **Conflict-evoked, not tonic:** Resting-state theta is unchanged between DBS ON and OFF; only task-induced conflict theta is modulated. CC gating is a triggered oscillatory mode switch, not a tonic background state.
- **Theta predicts clinical outcome:** VLPFC ON–OFF theta change (not RT change alone) predicts depression symptom improvement (r=0.76, AUC=0.87, n=8). A larger "theta rebound" with DBS OFF predicts worse outcome — interpreted as lack of neuroplastic remodeling.

## Limitations

- n=8 for EEG analysis (6/14 excluded for cap digitization failure or EMG artifact); small sample by clinical trial standards.
- Within-subjects crossover design (DBS ON then OFF); subjects and experimenters were not blind.
- Psychiatric population: MDD/OCD comorbidity, heterogeneous; no direct circuit proof (stimulation field anatomy not individually resolved).
