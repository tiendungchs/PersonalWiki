# Default mode network functional connectivity as a transdiagnostic biomarker of cognitive function

Tripathi V et al. (2025). *Biol Psychiatry Cogn Neurosci Neuroimaging* 10(4):359–368. [doi:10.1016/j.bpsc.2024.12.016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bpsc.2024.12.016)

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **DMN-FPN anti-correlation predicts attention:** Stronger DMN–FPN negative correlation → less variable behavioral performance on flanker task; replicated at large-N population neuroscience dataset; reduced attention problems index — a quantified behavioral consequence of network segregation, not just a correlational observation.
- **DMN-FPN cooperate during goal-directed cognition:** During episodic retrieval, semantic processing, creative idea evaluation, and goal-directed thought, DMN and FPN co-activate rather than anti-correlate; FPN contains sub-networks with differential connectivity (one correlated with DAN, another with DMN) — the simple antagonism model is wrong for internally-directed executive cognition.
- **CPM whole-brain attention signature:** Connectome-based Predictive Modeling (CPM) identifies whole-brain connectivity signatures for sustained attention (spanning DMN, FPN, visual, cerebellar, and motor networks) and for mind-wandering (DMN, somatomotor, DAN, ventral attention, visual, FPN); within-subject variation in mind-wandering is highly predicted by DMN anticorrelations.
- **Precision fMRI caveat:** Up to 62% of variance in group-level FC matrices is explained by cross-subject *spatial* variation in functional networks; group-level averages lose individual signal, especially in transmodal cortices (DMN, FPN); subject-level precision fMRI (multi-session) is the recommended path for reliable biomarker use.
- **GSR controversy:** Global signal regression increases anticorrelations by mean-centering; effects detectable only with GSR may still reflect genuine neural features (e.g., TMS-subgenual ACC treatment response associations were GSR-dependent but clinically validated); recommend reporting both with and without GSR.
- **Transdiagnostic DMN dysregulation:** Altered DMN FC appears across ASD, ADHD, AD, PD, MDD, and SZ — with distinct mechanistic signatures per disorder; convergence supports DMN FC as a broad biomarker of network health, warranting disorder-specific parsing.

## Limitations

- Review paper — no new empirical data; conclusions depend on heterogeneous original literature.
- Many clinical findings rely on group-level FC, subject to the 62% spatial variance confound; precision fMRI not yet standard in clinical populations.
- Network definition inconsistencies (FPN vs. CEN vs. CCN across studies) hamper cross-study comparison.
