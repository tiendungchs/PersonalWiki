# The Human Hippocampus: Cognitive Maps or Relational Memory?

**Citation:** Kumaran, D. & Maguire, E. A. (2005). *Journal of Neuroscience*, 25(31), 7254–7259. doi:10.1523/JNEUROSCI.1103-05.2005

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Spatial + relational combination is required for HC engagement.** HC activates only when both conditions are present simultaneously. Spatial nonrelational (imagining friends' buildings) and social relational (navigating friends' social network) both fail to engage HC; only spatial relational (navigating London between friends' houses) does.

- **Social and spatial graphs are topologically identical yet neurally dissociated.** Both tasks use the same 14 friends as nodes; edges are either spatial proximity or social acquaintance. Tasks matched on difficulty, reaction time, relational demands, and episodic-memory load. HC is absent from the social graph condition even vs. a low-level vowel-counting baseline.

- **Domain × task interaction uniquely localizes to hippocampus.** The factorial design (domain: spatial/social × task: relational/nonrelational) yields a statistically significant interaction only in right (and weakly left) posterior HC (peak: 30, −36, −3; z = 4.28). PHG, RSC, PPC activate for spatial tasks regardless of relational demand — they are the *spatial context* substrate; HC is the *spatial-relational* substrate.

- **Social relational processing engages mPFC, STS, TPJ, temporal poles.** These are mentalizing / theory-of-mind regions. The social graph activates the social cognition network, not the medial temporal memory network.

- **Supports cognitive map theory (O'Keefe & Nadel 1978) over relational theory (Eichenbaum 1993/2004).** Relational theory predicts equal HC engagement for matched relational tasks across domains; this prediction is directly refuted.

---

## Limitations

- Single fMRI paradigm; 18 participants; BOLD correlational not causal.
- "Social relational" ≠ all non-spatial reasoning. Dupret 2020, Bernardi 2020, and Courellis 2024 show HC CAN participate in non-spatial abstract inference — but those tasks share temporal-sequential structure with navigation. The Kumaran & Maguire result is specific to declarative associative graphs without metric or temporal embedding.
- "Spatial" task conflates familiarity (years in London) with spatial structure; spatial embedding is not experimentally isolated from other factors.
