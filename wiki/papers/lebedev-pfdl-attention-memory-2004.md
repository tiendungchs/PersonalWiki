# Representation of Attended Versus Remembered Locations in Prefrontal Cortex

Lebedev MA, Messinger A, Kralik JD, Wise SP. *PLoS Biology* 2(11):e365, 2004. PMID: 15510225.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Attentional dominance in PFdl**: 61% of spatially tuned PFdl neurons encode the currently *attended* location; only 16% encode the *remembered* location; 23% are hybrid (encode both). Directly refutes the Goldman-Rakic maintenance-memory theory as the sole account of PFdl delay-period activity.
- **Dynamic attentional tracking**: The attended-location representation grows stronger as the trigger signal approaches — ruling out a purely sensory account (identical sensory input early vs. late in the delay; spatial tuning increases late). PFdl encodes behavioral relevance, not just stimulus presence.
- **Regional specialization within dlPFC**: Ventrolateral PFdl is attention-dominant (attention 28%, memory 2%, hybrid 5%); dorsomedial PFdl is mixed (8%/9%/10%). The dlPFC hub is not functionally uniform — subregion structure matters.
- **Hybrid cells enable disambiguation**: Neurons with dissimilar preferred locations for attended vs. remembered signals resolve the ambiguity that unimodal cells cannot. The full population encodes both variables simultaneously; decoding the remembered location from attention cells (and vice versa) is above chance, confirming a continuum rather than discrete classes.
- **Smooth post-response transition**: After the saccade, the representation of the now-fixated location grows while the formerly relevant location fades — PFdl tracks behavioral relevance dynamically, not a static buffer of past inputs.

## Limitations

- Non-human primate only (rhesus macaque); spatial modality only — unclear if attentional dominance extends to non-spatial or abstract content.
- Attention vs. anticipatory motor preparation not fully dissociated; some attention cells may partly reflect motor planning to the attended location.
- Covert-attention paradigm; overt fixation of the attended stimulus might yield different proportions.
