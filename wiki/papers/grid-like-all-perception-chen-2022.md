# Are Grid-Like Representations a Component of All Perception and Cognition?

**Chen ZS, Zhang X, Long X, Zhang S-J (2022). *Frontiers in Neural Circuits* 16:924016.** Opinion / perspective article — speculative, not experimental.

## Key computational insights

- **Empirical extension beyond HC-EC:** grid cells recorded in rat **primary somatosensory cortex (S1**, hindlimb area, L4–VI) and **secondary visual cortex (V2**, V2M) (Long & Zhang 2021; Long et al. 2021a). These sensory-cortical grid cells (i) show conjunctive head-direction coding, (ii) **persist under whisker-trimming and darkness** (spatial modulation is sensory-input-independent), and (iii) are **sparse and heterogeneous** — not densely tiled in one layer like MEC.
- **Universality thesis:** grid-like coding is proposed as a *general solution to "spatial localization"* across **all** sensory and higher-order cortex, arising from a **generalized path-integration principle** driven by the perception-action loop. Non-spatial/abstract input replaces physical space with an "attribute space."
- **Generic emergence mechanism:** grids fall out of either **SR eigenvectors** (low-dim sparse map; Stachenfeld 2017) *or* **RNN recurrent attractors** (ring/torus; Sorscher 2020, Zhang 2022) — same code from two routes. Aligns with Thousand-Brains cortical-column reference frames (Hawkins) and Hinton's capsule coordinate frames.
- **"Dynamic resource allocation":** only a task-context-dependent *subset* of sensory neurons is recruited for grid-like reference-frame computation.
- **Four testable predictions:** (1) auditory-cortex grid cells; (2) human S1/V2 grid codes (fMRI, VR); (3) frontal-cortex grid cells in animals; (4) a **universal egocentric→allocentric transformation** (thalamus as multiplexer via theta/gamma bursting).

## Limitations (its value as a skeptic — Axis-3 devil's advocate)

- **Detection is an "arbitrary threshold phenomenon":** spatial autocorrelation generates **false positives**; needs strict shuffle controls (Barry & Burgess 2007).
- **fMRI-BOLD ≠ spiking:** abstract-domain grid codes are BOLD-only and have **never been single-unit-confirmed in humans**.
- **Contrived-task dependence:** human abstract-space tasks impose *very few* feature dimensions (≈2D); the authors concede "the lack of structure in tasks brings a difficulty of detecting generalized grid codes" — unknown whether grid codes survive in less-constrained cognition.
- Purely speculative; the authors note predictions in this field "often proven wrong."
