- (tentative) Used the Janelia hemibrain electron-microscopy connectome to map complete synaptic wiring of the *Drosophila* head direction ring attractor circuit, providing ground-truth connectivity for the functional dynamics observed by Seelig & Jayaraman 2015.
- (tentative) E-PG neurons form a ring with local excitatory connections and global inhibitory feedback mediated by P-EG neurons — the anatomical implementation of the cosine-like connectivity matrix predicted by ring attractor theory.
- (tentative) P-EN neurons (path integrators) receive angular velocity input and synapse asymmetrically onto neighboring E-PG neurons consistent with the rotation direction — implementing the bump-shift mechanism at the synaptic level.
