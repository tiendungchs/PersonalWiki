# The Faculty of Language: What Is It, Who Has It, and How Did It Evolve?

Hauser MD, Chomsky N, Fitch WT. *Science* 298:1569–1579, 2002.

Foundational manifesto splitting the language faculty into **FLB** (broad: sensory-motor + conceptual-intentional + recursion) and **FLN** (narrow: recursion alone). Its computational content is directly reusable as a theory of the missing combinatorial engine in a reasoning model.

## Key computational insights

- **Recursion is the uniquely-human core (Hypothesis 3).** Almost all of FLB is shared with other animals (continuity is the null hypothesis); only FLN — a recursive operator yielding **discrete infinity** (finite elements → unbounded discrete expressions) — is qualitatively new. Sharpens "be compositional" into "acquire one recursive combinator." → [[wiki/concepts/recursion.md]]
- **Chomsky hierarchy as a probe.** Finite-state/regular (local, transitional-probability) grammars are provably insufficient for natural language; a phrase-structure/context-free grammar (long-distance hierarchical embedding) is the minimum. **AₙBₙ vs. ABₙ (Fitch & Hauser):** tamarins learn the finite-state ABₙ but fail the context-free AₙBₙ; humans learn both. → [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]] (meta-graph expressiveness), [[wiki/concepts/compositional-generalization.md]]
- **Statistical learning is not the special ingredient.** Transitional-probability word segmentation (Saffran) is available to infants, tamarins, and across modalities — a finite-state-floor capacity, not FLN.
- **Domain-general recursion hypothesis.** FLN may have evolved for **number, navigation, or social relations**, not communication; comparative work should look for recursion outside language. A modular, domain-specific recursion becoming *penetrable/domain-general* is proposed as the human leap.
- **Discrete infinity ≅ natural numbers.** Both generated by a successor-style operator; children's successor-function "aha" vs. chimps' one-by-one numeral learning distinguishes generative from associative number. → [[wiki/concepts/core-knowledge.md]]
- **Learnability argument (poverty of the stimulus).** Gold's theorem + Nowak: infinitely many I-languages fit any finite data; no domain-general learner suffices without innate constraints ("universal grammar"). Motivates constrained search / inductive bias in [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]].

## Limitations

Programmatic, not empirical — Hypothesis 3 is explicitly "tentative and testable." "Recursion" is left informally specified (no mechanistic model). The FLN-is-only-recursion claim is contested (Pinker & Jackendoff 2005); the AₙBₙ tamarin result has been challenged on whether it truly tests context-free competence vs. counting heuristics.
