# What Makes Human Cognition Unique?

Michael Tomasello & Hannes Rakoczy, *Mind & Language* 18(2):121–147, 2003 (repr. *Intellectica* 46–47, 2007).

The source of MacLean's "desert-island thought experiment" ([[wiki/papers/maclean-human-cognition-evolution-2016.md]]): an individual human raised without culture ≈ a great ape. Human-level competence (language, math, institutions) is a **collective cultural product**, not individual brainpower — so the uniquely human trait is the machinery for *pooling* cognitive resources, not a bigger solitary reasoner.

## Key computational insights (for building a reasoning model)

- **Two ontogenetic steps, one adaptation.** (1) ~1 yr: understanding persons as **intentional agents** → *shared intentionality*. (2) ~4 yr: understanding **mental agents** with false beliefs → *collective intentionality*. Step 1 is the biologically-specified leap (culture-invariant onset, autism disrupts it early); step 2 is an ontogenetic *construction* requiring ~years of linguistic interaction — **no dedicated biological substrate of its own**. Design reading: the meta-representation stage is *learned/bootstrapped*, not innate.
- **Three signatures of the human capacity**: (i) **sharedness** (self-other equivalence — same goal-directed act under one description "I do what you did"), (ii) **perspective** (one entity under multiple construals — car/vehicle/SUV), (iii) **normativity** (reflective stance: a use can be right/wrong).
- **Imitation vs. emulation** — the acquisition-channel distinction. *Emulation* (apes, human infants): learn *about the object* from watching, devise own means. *Imitation* (uniquely human): copy the **intentional structure/goal** — learn *through* the demonstrator. Imitation reproduces intended (not accidental, not merely-attempted) acts → brings self and other under the same description.
- **Linguistic bootstrapping of meta-representation** (Lohmann & Tomasello): two *independent* facilitators of false-belief understanding — (a) perspective-shifting discourse, (b) **sentential complement syntax** ("X thinks that p"), a recursive embedding that supplies the representational *format* for beliefs. Mastery is gradual (formulaic "I think" → full propositional-attitude paradigm). **Representational redescription** (Karmiloff-Smith): implicit competence expressed in language, then redescribed in that same language → explicit symbolic concepts.
- **Apes have the substrate, not the graft.** Chimps read intention-in-action (unwilling vs. unable) and track what a competitor has/hasn't seen — but show no sharedness/perspective/normativity: no pointing-to-share, no pretend play, no self-other equivalence.

## Limitations

- Developmental/comparative psychology; no computational model or circuit mechanism.
- Social-cognition framing; the mapping to non-social abstract reasoning is inferential (systemizing axis — see MacLean).
- The "language bootstraps belief" causal claim rests on correlational + small training studies.
