# Conscious Processing and the Global Neuronal Workspace Hypothesis

**Citation:** Mashour GA, Roelfsema P, Changeux JP, Dehaene S. (2020). Neuron 105(5), 776–798. doi: 10.1016/j.neuron.2020.01.026

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Non-linear ignition as the WM entry gate:** Conscious access occurs when information crosses a threshold and ignites a self-sustained, reverberant PFC-parietal network (~300ms post-stimulus, P300/late-positive ERP). This is a phase transition: subliminal stimuli produce decaying activity waves; consciously perceived stimuli produce sudden coherent network-wide co-activation. The ignition threshold maps directly onto SDT's detection threshold (van Vugt et al. 2018); pre-stimulus PFC firing rate predicts sensitivity (d'); spontaneous PFC ignitions drive false alarms.

- **AMPA/NMDA feedforward/feedback dissociation:** The feedforward sweep (first ~200ms) runs on fast AMPA receptors and is preserved under anesthesia, sleep, and in unconscious states. Recurrent ignition depends on slow NMDA receptors — confirmed by ketamine selectively abolishing ignition while leaving feedforward responses intact. Layer-specific: superficial layers (II/III) carry bottom-up gamma; deep layers (V) sustain top-down alpha-beta recurrence. Provides a receptor-level mechanism for the predictive coding feedforward/feedback split.

- **Active vs. activity-silent WM:** Only items in GNW ignition state (globally broadcasted, sustained firing) can be mentally transformed — rotated, sequenced, or reasoned over. Activity-silent items (STSP-style synaptic traces) bridge delays but are not transformable until reinstated into active firing. MEG evidence (Trübutschek et al. 2017): activity-silent WM produces no decodable ignition signature. Trübutschek et al. 2019: whenever WM content must be transformed, active firing reemerges. This is the functional distinction between a *store* and a *workspace*.

- **Bow-tie bottleneck hub architecture:** The GNW is a central core of densely interconnected long-range neurons in PFC + inferior parietal + anterior temporal + cingulate + precuneus, confirmed as a "high-efficiency cortical core" by NHP tracer studies (Markov et al. 2013). This hub routes information between specialized modular processors without O(N²) direct pairwise connections. Dynamic functional connectivity within this hub is dramatically richer in the conscious state than anatomical connectivity alone permits.

- **Dynamic state repertoire as the consciousness signature:** All classes of general anesthetics (propofol, sevoflurane, ketamine — different molecular targets) equivalently reduce the brain's functional connectivity diversity to a pattern crystallized to the anatomical matrix. Conscious processing = exploring a rich meta-graph of routing configurations above the anatomical baseline. The perturbational complexity index (TMS + EEG) measures this repertoire and discriminates vegetative/minimally-conscious/waking states, consistent with both GNW and IIT predictions.

---

## Limitations

- Scope: access consciousness (global information availability) only; phenomenal consciousness (qualia) deferred.
- Posterior "hot zone" (IIT) vs. GNW frontal-parietal core debate empirically unresolved for tasks emphasizing fine-grained visual processing.
- Self-consciousness and recursive thought identified as important extensions but mechanistically underspecified.
