# Arbitrary associations between antecedents and actions

Wise, S.P. & Murray, E.A. (2000). *Trends in Neurosciences* 23(6):271–276. doi:10.1016/S0166-2236(00)01570-8. Review of ablation, neuroimaging, and single-unit evidence for arbitrary visuomotor mapping in monkeys and humans.

Full treatment in [[wiki/concepts/arbitrary-mapping.md]].

## Key computational insights

- **Arbitrary ("nonstandard") mapping is the type where the cue's location neither constrains nor determines the action** — distinct from standard mapping (cue = target) and transformational mapping (cue location feeds an algorithm). Liberating action from spatial information is what permits action choice on *prevailing context*.
- **Asymmetric lesion dissociation:** hippocampectomy impairs *novel* mappings while sparing pre-lesion ones; **PMd lesions impair both**. HC drops out with overlearning; premotor does not. Spared old mappings rule out perceptual/motor explanations of the deficit.
- **Binding rate:** experienced monkeys learn 24 new mappings/day — substantial learning by ~3 trials/cue, <10% error by ~8 trials/cue. A concrete benchmark for fast rebinding of an arbitrary action alphabet.
- **~50% of PFC delay-period neurons code the cue×action conjunction** vs. ~30% cue and ~20% action direction (Miller, mapping-reversal design) — the *mapping*, not its terms, is the represented variable.
- **Two intermingled learning signals** (SEF/premotor): learning-dependent activity rises, learning-selective activity falls to silence. Directional preference rules out reward rate/arousal/motivation confounds; fixation + stereotyped saccades rule out cue-position and kinematic confounds.
- **Regional division:** PFC is the general arbitrary-mapping engine (only convergence of cue + action + outcome); premotor is visuomotor-specific (not visuovisual); striatum differentiates novel/familiar mappings (~30% of neurons) with changes concentrated immediately post-cue; HC changes are weak and learning-*selective* (tentatively, a sparse-coding signature).
- The HC lesions reproduced **H.M.**'s amnesia in monkeys — unintentionally, as a comparison group for an abandoned amygdala hypothesis.

## Limitations

Review of studies too heterogeneous in design for direct cross-area comparison (the authors flag this explicitly for the HC-vs-premotor effect-size gap, making the sparse/dense coding explanation speculation). The visuomotor / visuospatial / visuovisual decomposition is unexplored systematically. Cerebellar involvement reported in humans, disputed in monkeys, and set aside. Predates COIN/MOSAIC — offers no computational model of how the binding is selected or inferred.
