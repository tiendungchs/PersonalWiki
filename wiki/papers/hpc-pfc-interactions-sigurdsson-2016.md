# Hippocampal-Prefrontal Interactions in Cognition, Behavior and Psychiatric Disease

Sigurdsson & Duvarci, *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience*, 2016. Review of HC-PFC interactions across cognition and schizophrenia.

---

## Key computational insights

- **Direct anatomy**: vHPC → mPFC is a monosynaptic glutamatergic projection; the reverse (ACC → dHPC CA1/CA3) also exists and is causally required for spatial memory retrieval (Rajasethupathy 2015). Nucleus Reuniens (NR) of the thalamus provides an indirect relay connecting both structures via theta-band coordination.
- **Theta/gamma dissociation in spatial WM**: gamma synchrony (30–80 Hz) between vHPC and PFC peaks during the *sample/encoding* phase; theta synchrony (4–12 Hz) peaks during the *choice/retrieval* phase. The two phases rely on mechanistically distinct circuits.
- **Causal evidence (optogenetics)**: silencing vHPC axon terminals in mPFC abolishes gamma synchrony and goal-specific PFC coding during encoding, but does **not** affect theta synchrony during retrieval — confirming direct vHPC input drives gamma while theta may depend on indirect paths (NR, dHPC).
- **Phase locking as information channel**: PFC neurons phase-locked to hippocampal theta show better spatial coding; degree of phase locking predicts behavioral performance. Synchrony is not epiphenomenal — it is the carrier of spatial information from HC to PFC.
- **Division of labor**: HC encodes spatial content during the sample phase (via gamma-band input to PFC); PFC accumulates this into a choice decision using theta-synchronized HC activity. Maps onto the HC one-step look-ahead / PFC outcome translation split established in Dupret 2020.

## Limitations

- Review paper; primary empirical findings cite rodent electrophysiology (Jones & Wilson 2005; Sigurdsson et al. 2010; Spellman et al. 2015). Human fMRI connectivity studies operate at a vastly different timescale (~0.1 Hz vs. 4–80 Hz), making direct comparison difficult.
- Schizophrenia findings (disrupted HC-PFC theta coherence in multiple genetic models) are included here but outside the primary reasoning-model scope of this wiki.
