# Yoo et al. 2022 — C2C State Transformation

Yoo K, Rosenberg MD, Kwon YH, Scheinost D, Constable RT, Chun MM. *NeuroImage* 257:119279, 2022. PMID: 35577026. HCP S1200 (316 unrelated subjects, 7 cognitive tasks).

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Rest-to-task as a learnable linear map**: PCA (subsystem extraction, 100 components) + PLS regression (state transformation, 10 latents) predicts individual task-specific functional connectomes from resting-state FC; accuracy r = 0.643–0.723 across 7 tasks, significantly better than resting-state baseline.
- **Task-general vs task-specific decomposition**: ~6 of 25 major PCA components have similar PLS coefficients across all 7 task states (task-general); later components, especially frontoparietal and frontal, change differently per task (task-specific); component 6 (DMN within-network connectivity + cross-network coupling) is the clearest task-general element.
- **Individual fingerprint amplified**: C2C-generated task connectomes have higher within-individual than cross-individual similarity; task engagement amplifies, not erases, individual uniqueness in functional connectivity.
- **Behavior prediction boost**: generated task connectomes outperform resting-state alone at predicting fluid intelligence (WM task: r = 0.180 vs. r = 0.076); 100-subject C2C ≥ 300-subject resting-state baseline.
- **74% state identification accuracy**: generated connectomes correctly identify which of 7 tasks they represent (vs. 14.3% chance) via fingerprinting, confirming each task has a distinct learned transformation signature.

## Limitations

- Linear model (PCA + PLS) misses nonlinear dynamics and network merging/splitting during task engagement.
- Forces one-to-one subsystem correspondence across states; true reconfiguration may be more flexible.
- Healthy adults 22–36 (HCP) only; generalizability to clinical populations untested.
- Does not cleanly separate task-evoked connectivity from task coactivation (spurious inflation).
