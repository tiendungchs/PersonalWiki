# The hippocampal–prefrontal pathway: The weak link in psychiatric disorders?

Spedding & Jay, *European Neuropsychopharmacology* 23(10): 1165–1181, 2013. doi:10.1016/j.euroneuro.2012.10.018

Review arguing that disruption of the monosynaptic vHPC→mPFC projection (H-PFC pathway) is a common pathophysiological substrate across schizophrenia, major depression, and PTSD.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Interneuron-mediated inhibition as primary mechanism**: The H-PFC pathway regulates mPFC primarily by modulating interneuron-mediated feed-forward inhibition of pyramidal neurons in PL — not direct excitation. VH stimulation produces short-latency AMPA-mediated excitation then rapid pyramidal inhibition via GABAergic interneurons. This inhibitory dominance is a gain-normalizing mechanism stabilizing PFC output under variable hippocampal drive.
- **D1 dopamine regulation of H-PFC LTP**: LTP/LTD in the H-PFC pathway is NMDA-dependent, PKA-dependent, and critically gated by D1 receptors. D1 activation increases mPFC interneuron excitability; dopamine administration to PFC increases HC-PFC theta-coherence. Stress disrupts D1-gated LTP → WM failure; antidepressants, glucocorticoid receptor antagonists, and clozapine each restore LTP. Extends the D1=WM-stability mapping beyond PFC-internal dynamics to the incoming HC-PFC channel itself.
- **Stress as H-PFC pathway disruptor**: Acute and chronic stress specifically block LTP in the vHPC→mPFC pathway. Molecular substrate: stress downregulates MEK/MAPK signaling and BDNF levels; chronic stress additionally produces mPFC neuron atrophy. The H-PFC pathway is the stress-sensitive bottleneck of the corticolimbic network — its failure simultaneously disrupts WM and emotional regulation because it serves both (via PL fear-expression vs. IL fear-inhibition arms).
- **Task-demand theta scaling**: PFC neurons with the highest task-related firing rate show the strongest coupling to hippocampal theta — synchrony preferentially recruits the most engaged PFC ensembles, not a uniform broadcast. Cognitive demand upregulates HC-PFC channel gain selectively.
- **Default mode network at rest**: Hippocampus and mPFC couple at rest as DMN components, consistent with constructing self-relevant mental simulations (prospective thought, social cognition). Resting-state HC-mPFC coupling disrupted across schizophrenia, depression, and PTSD — a transdiagnostic fMRI signature.

## Limitations

- Review paper; primary mechanistic evidence from rodent electrophysiology (anesthetized preparations); stress models use acute elevated-platform paradigm with unresolved generalizability to chronic human stress.
- Psychiatric disorder correlations are observational; the causal direction of HC-mPFC coupling disruption (cause vs. consequence of symptoms) is unresolved.
- The paper predates optogenetic dissection of the direct vHPC→mPFC vs. indirect NR relay contributions — its mechanistic conclusions are complemented by Sigurdsson 2016's causal dissociation.
