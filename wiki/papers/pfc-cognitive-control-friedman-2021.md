# The Role of Prefrontal Cortex in Cognitive Control and Executive Function

**Citation:** Friedman, N.P. & Robbins, T.W. (2021). *Nature Neuropsychopharmacology*, 47, 1–23. doi:10.1038/s41386-021-01132-0

---

## Five Key Computational Insights

- **Goal maintenance as the unified CC (Cognitive Control) mechanism.** Miller & Cohen (2001): PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) actively maintains goal representations that send top-down *bias signals* to downstream regions, enabling weak but contextually correct stimulus-response mappings to out-compete stronger habitual ones via lateral inhibition. This is the biological grounding for what Block 3D (goal generator) computes — active maintenance is the mechanism, not just a property.

- **Hierarchical lateral PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) maps directly to Block 3C.** A well-confirmed rostro-caudal gradient: caudal PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) (BA-8) = immediate stimulus-response conditional mappings; mid-lateral PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) (BA-9/46) = contextual rule maintenance and n-back monitoring; frontopolar PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) (BA-10) = branching rule contingencies held in WM (rules about rules). TMS-causal evidence confirms the hierarchy is directional (mid-lateral integrates caudal and rostral signals), not just correlational.

- **Three dissociable CC (Cognitive Control) components.** Miyake et al. (2000) latent variable analysis: Inhibition / response inhibition (≈ Common CC), WM Updating, Mental Set-Shifting. Latent factor correlations r = 0.42–0.63 (unity) but separable (diversity). The inhibition factor completely absorbs the Common CC (Cognitive Control) factor — individual differences in CC (Cognitive Control) reduce to individual differences in goal maintenance (response inhibition specific variance is absent in the bifactor model).

- **ACC as unsigned prediction error (Predicted Response Outcome model).** ACC (Anterior Cingulate Cortex) computes *unsigned* prediction errors at multiple timescales — both unexpected omissions and unexpected occurrences. This modulates *reactive CC* (error-correction) and *proactive CC* (anticipatory bias adjustment before conflict). Distinct from DA/TD error: unsigned, multi-timescale, and modulates control rather than value.

- **Neurochemical CC (Cognitive Control) specificity.** Dopamine D1 receptor activation in dlPFC stabilizes WM (inverted-U: too little → noise, too much → loss of signal). 5-HT specifically modulates OFC (Orbitofrontal Cortex) reversal learning, not set-shifting (OFC serotonin depletion impairs reversal; vlPFC lesion impairs extra-dimensional shift). NA (Noradrenaline / Norepinephrine) via RIFG hyperdirect projection to STN (Subthalamic Nucleus) mediates response inhibition (atomoxetine enhances stop-signal; citalopram / SSRI does not). ACh modulates "cognitive effort" allocation.

---

## Limitations

- Primarily neuroimaging and lesion data; mechanistic circuit account of goal-maintenance/biasing is missing.
- The computational implementation of the Common CC (Cognitive Control) factor (goal maintenance vs. inhibition) remains debated — these accounts are not operationally distinguished.
- Clinical sections (p factor, psychopathology) and developmental data carry no reasoning-model implications and are omitted here.
