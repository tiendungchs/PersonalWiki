# Unraveling the evolution of uniquely human cognition

Evan L. MacLean, *PNAS* 113(23):6348–6354, 2016.

Phylogenetic-comparative synthesis of what makes human cognition unique and *how/when/why* it evolved. Read here as a strategic argument about **which brain to model** for a reasoning system — and why "brain-inspired" underspecifies the target.

## Key insights (for building a reasoning model)

- **Uniqueness = synergy, not a single module.** Human cognition depends on the interaction of **representational** traits (theory of mind, shared/"we" intentionality) *and* **motivational** traits (prosocial, cooperative drive). "Unlikely to be accounted for by changes to any singular cognitive system." → argues against the *scale-one-module* thesis; the target may be a *constellation* whose members are individually insufficient.
- **No animal has the full constellation, but its parts are scattered across taxa** as homologies and — more usefully — **analogies from convergent evolution.** So "model the brain" is underdetermined: choosing *which* brain (or which mix of convergent solutions) is itself a design decision. Complements the spatial-coding case in [[wiki/concepts/convergent-allocentric-coding.md]].
- **Systemizing vs. empathizing axis (Baron-Cohen).** *Systemizing* = analyzing the world as lawful, deterministic rules (spatial cognition, tool use, causal reasoning) — the abstract-reasoning-relevant axis; chimpanzees lead here. *Empathizing* = theory-of-mind/social; bonobos lead. Reduced prenatal androgen shifts the balance toward empathizing. **Implication:** the rule-extraction ("systemizing") capacity is at least partly *dissociable* from the social stack — a reasoning model need not reproduce full human sociality.
- **Cumulative culture is the actual engine.** An individual human raised without culture ≈ a great ape (Tomasello/Rakoczy desert-island thought experiment; see [[wiki/concepts/cultural-learning.md]] for the acquisition mechanism). Human "calculus" is cultural accretion + social learning (gaze-following, shared attention, language), not a bigger solitary reasoner. → much human-level competence is *offloaded* to a cultural ratchet (external memory + transmission), relevant to test-time compute, tool use, and multi-agent designs.
- **Absolute (not relative) brain size ↔ self-control** across 36 vertebrate species (MacLean et al. 2014) → inhibition/cognitive-control scales with absolute compute; social group size did *not* predict self-control (but did predict perspective-taking in lemurs). See [[wiki/concepts/cognitive-control.md]].
- **Self-domestication / neoteny as a developmental lever.** Bonobos: selection against aggression → altered developmental *timing* (heterochrony) → increased tolerance → unlocks cooperation and social learning. A parallel "second wave" of craniofacial feminization (~200 kya) coincides with behavioral modernity. See [[wiki/concepts/neoteny.md]].

## Limitations

- Evolutionary scenarios are explicitly speculative, drawn from few (phylogenetically informative) taxa; correlational, not mechanistic at the circuit level.
- No computational model; provides framing and constraints, not an architecture.
- Human-specific social framing; the mapping to non-social abstract reasoning is inferential (this page's systemizing-axis reading).
