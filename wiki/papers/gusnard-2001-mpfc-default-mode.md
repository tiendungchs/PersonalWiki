# Medial prefrontal cortex and self-referential mental activity

Gusnard DA, Akbudak E, Shulman GL, Raichle ME. *PNAS* 2001. doi:10.1073/pnas.071043098

Companion fMRI paper to Raichle et al. 2001 (which coined "default mode" from PET OEF). Provides the first explicit experimental dissociation of dorsal vs. ventral MPFC within the default state, and ties dorsal MPFC activity to self-referential mental content.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Dorsal MPFC (BA 8/9/10) is the self-referential simulation engine.** In a blocked fMRI design (N=24), internally cued judgments (ICC: "how does this picture make you feel?") drive greater dorsal MPFC activity than externally cued judgments (ECC: "indoors or outdoors?"). Same region correlates with stimulus-independent thought (SIT) count at rest — it is the seat of daydreams, self-generated thought, and spontaneous inner monologue.
- **Ventral MPFC (BA 24/25/32) instantiates ongoing emotional/salience monitoring.** Decreases relative to visual fixation in BOTH active task conditions, regardless of whether the task is self-referential or external. Attenuation by cognitive load is the functional signature — emotional monitoring is the default content suppressed by task engagement.
- **Default state = behavioral simulation.** Following Ingvar (1974), spontaneous MPFC activity is continuous inner rehearsal and optimization of cognitive/behavioral programs — a narrative self with integrated access to past, present, and anticipated future. This is not noise; it is the active content of the resting brain.
- **Baseline selection determines apparent activation direction.** Using an attention-demanding control task as baseline (common in the literature) makes ventral MPFC appear to "activate" for some tasks — but this is a decrease-in-baseline artifact. Visual fixation reveals the true direction (down, not up) and is the methodologically correct default state reference.

## Limitations

- 1.5T BOLD fMRI; spatial resolution limits precise delineation of dorsal/ventral MPFC subregions.
- Single affective-picture paradigm; ICC judgment conflates self-reference with emotional processing; generalization to non-affective introspective tasks is inferred.
- No lesion or stimulation evidence; causal necessity of dorsal MPFC for SITs is inferred from correlational PET and the present fMRI data.
