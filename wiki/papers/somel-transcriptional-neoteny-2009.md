# Transcriptional neoteny in the human brain — Somel et al. 2009

*PNAS 106(14):5743–5748. doi:10.1073/pnas.0900544106.* Genome-wide mRNA (~8,000 genes) in prefrontal cortex (DLPFC + SFG) of human, chimpanzee, and rhesus macaque across postnatal life; macaque as outgroup to polarize lineage-specific shifts. The primary molecular evidence for the neoteny hypothesis that [[wiki/papers/sherwood-natural-history-mind-2008.md]] cites secondhand.

## Key computational insights

- **Development dominates expression variance.** Age explains **29%** of total expression variance, species **17%**, sex **<2%** (n.s.). 71% of expressed genes change significantly with age; ~50% of the total newborn→40yr change happens in the **first year** — postnatal wiring is where the action is.
- **Direct transcriptomic neoteny.** A heterochrony test (best age-shift fitting one species' trajectory onto the other) + macaque polarization assigns genes to human/chimp × neoteny/acceleration. **Human-neotenic is ~2× enriched (38% of assignable genes)** and reproduces across two PFC regions and array platforms: adult human PFC expression resembles that of **younger chimpanzees**.
- **Mosaic, not global (~4%).** The neotenic shift affects only a limited gene subset; simulations exclude a whole-transcriptome slowdown. **Design consequence:** the "extended plastic window" is a *targeted schedule shift on specific gene networks*, not a uniform developmental slowing — argues for **per-subsystem plasticity schedules**.
- **Which genes: gray-matter / synaptic.** Human-neotenic genes are overrepresented among **gray-matter-specific** genes and growth/development processes (cell adhesion, synaptic transmission, axonogenesis) — i.e. the *synaptic/structural* machinery, exactly the substrate of slow-W consolidation.
- **When: adolescence / pruning phase.** Human–chimp divergence for neotenic genes peaks **~10 yr (early adolescence)**, coinciding with the gray-matter-volume decline attributed to **synaptic elimination (pruning)**. The neotenic delay is concentrated at the *consolidation/pruning* stage near sexual maturity → delayed freezing of structure, extending the active-learning window.

## Limitations

Bulk microarray (no cell-type resolution; gray/white ratio differs between the two datasets); small chimp/macaque n (9–14); postmortem tissue. The heterochrony test conflates onset-shift vs. rate-shift (hence the umbrella term "neoteny"); it also under-detects real shifts, so 4% is a floor. Correlation between expression timing and cognition is inferential, not causal.
