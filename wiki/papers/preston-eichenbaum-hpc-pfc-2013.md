# Interplay of hippocampus and prefrontal cortex in memory

Preston & Eichenbaum, *Current Biology* 23(17): R764–R773, 2013. doi:10.1016/j.cub.2013.05.041

Review synthesizing rodent + human evidence on the HC–mPFC circuit for memory encoding, schema formation, and consolidation.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Bidirectional HC↔mPFC circuit:** Ventral/anterior HC → mPFC (contextual representations, direct monosynaptic); mPFC → perirhinal/lateral EC → dorsal/posterior HC (context-guided selective retrieval). Ventral HC encodes generalized context; dorsal HC encodes specific events at specific locations.
- **Division of labor across memory stages** (Table 1): HC links associative elements (A-B, B-C) and employs invariant representations to link overlapping elements during consolidation; mPFC reconciles conflicts in overlapping associations during learning, creates schematic organizations during consolidation, and selects the correct schema during expression.
- **Associative inference requires both structures:** HC lesions block A-C transitive inference even when A-B and B-C pairs are individually learned; mPFC lesions also impair inference but via loss of conflict resolution and schema expression rather than integration per se.
- **Schema accelerates HC encoding:** When a schema exists, new overlapping associations are acquired in a single trial with immediate mPFC engagement (Tse et al.). Without a pre-existing schema, HC encodes independently and mPFC is recruited only after remote consolidation.
- **vmPFC-HC coupling predicts inference:** In human fMRI, vmPFC-HC coupling increases with each repeated presentation of overlapping associations (A-B, B-C). The degree of vmPFC engagement during overlapping-pair learning predicts subsequent A-C inferential performance.
- **Time cells:** HC maps temporal organization of episodes via "time cells" that fire at sequential moments; complements place cell spatial coding; required for memory for event order.
- **Consolidation = schema formation, not transfer:** New memories are assimilated into pre-existing schematic networks and the networks are modified via accommodation — not simply copied to cortex.

## Limitations

- Review paper (2013); primary empirical findings rely on rodent behavioral paradigms (odor-place, fear conditioning, transitive inference). Direct cellular mechanisms in humans remain incompletely characterized.
- Dorsal/ventral HC distinction (posterior/anterior in humans) has substantial individual variability; fMRI resolution limits precision of the functional subdivision.
- Cellular mechanism of mPFC schema accommodation — how mPFC triggers selective schema modification without overwriting unaffected associations — is unspecified.
