# Prefrontal-Hippocampal Interactions in Memory and Emotion

Jin & Maren, *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience* 9:170, 2015. doi:10.3389/fnsys.2015.00170

Review of direct and indirect HC-mPFC anatomical pathways and their roles in working memory, episodic memory, contextual retrieval, emotional memory, and psychiatric disorders.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **Direct HPC→mPFC pathway**: ventral CA1 and subiculum → IL/PL cortices (rat); CA1/subiculum → medial orbital/medial frontal cortex (primate). Monosynaptic glutamatergic; AMPA-receptor dependent; exhibits LTP, LTD, and depotentiation (NMDA-dependent). The primary channel for spatial content transmission during the WM encoding phase (Spellman et al. 2015; gamma-band carrier).
- **Indirect mPFC→RE→HPC pathway**: mPFC → thalamic nucleus reuniens (RE) → HPC (hippocampus). Single RE neurons send collaterals to *both* HC and mPFC simultaneously, placing RE as an active bidirectional coordinator. RE lesions impair spatial WM (radial arm maze; DNMTP task); mPFC→RE→sHPC pathway is causally required for future path representation during goal-directed navigation (Ito et al. 2015).
- **Tripartite motivational circuit**: ventral HC (VH) neurons projecting to *both* mPFC and amygdala are preferentially recruited during fear renewal after extinction — not simple HC→mPFC context delivery, but a dual-projection that simultaneously modulates the cognitive controller (mPFC) and the emotional valuation module (amygdala). VH thereby gates which arm of the mPFC-amygdala reciprocal circuit (PL→fear-expression vs. IL→fear-inhibition) wins.
- **NAcc as convergence zone**: nucleus accumbens receives convergent synaptic inputs from PFC, HPC, and amygdala; mediates goal-directed behavior by integrating HC-dependent contextual information + amygdala-dependent emotional/motivational information + PFC cognitive information. The NAcc is the biological motivation-routing hub.
- **Extinction memory is context-bound**: extinction does not erase fear; both memories coexist and context-dependent HPC-mPFC gating determines which is retrieved. Fear renewal (fear outside extinction context) is abolished by VH-mPFC disconnection — the context signal from HPC is the switch.
- **Synaptic plasticity as emotional memory substrate**: extinction learning potentiates VH-evoked potentials in mPFC (LTP); chronic stress blocks this potentiation → impaired extinction; BDNF in VH-IL pathway and histone acetylation in HPC-IL network modulate extinction encoding.

## Limitations

- Review paper; primary empirical findings are rodent electrophysiology and lesion studies. Human evidence relies on EEG coherence and fMRI connectivity — different timescales and spatial resolution.
- Fear conditioning is used as the primary emotional memory model; generalization to other motivational valences (reward, curiosity) not directly addressed.
- Psychiatric disorder data (schizophrenia, PTSD, depression) are correlational; causal direction of HC-mPFC dysregulation is unresolved.
