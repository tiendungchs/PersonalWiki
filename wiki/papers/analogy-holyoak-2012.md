# Analogy and Relational Reasoning — Holyoak (2012)

Holyoak, K. J. (2012). Analogy and relational reasoning. In K. J. Holyoak & R. G. Morrison (Eds.), *The Oxford handbook of thinking and reasoning* (pp. 234–259). Oxford University Press.

---

## Five Key Computational Insights

- **Four-component algorithmic decomposition:** Retrieval (surface-biased LTM access) → Mapping (constraint-satisfaction over structural + semantic + pragmatic) → Inference (CWSG: copy-with-substitution-and-generation over unmapped source propositions) → Schema Induction (abstract relational category from two compared analogs). The retrieval gap is the critical failure mode: people who can map an analog once both analogs are in WM nonetheless fail to retrieve the structurally correct analog from LTM (~12% remote retrieval vs. ~86% correct mapping once cued).

- **Multiconstraint theory and coherent mapping:** Good mappings simultaneously maximize structural parallelism, semantic similarity of corresponding elements, and pragmatic centrality (goal-relevant elements prioritized). These constraints are adjudicated by constraint satisfaction, not a strict hierarchy. When constraints conflict, bistable mappings arise (e.g., Persian Gulf–WWII analogy: either US≡US/Bush≡FDR or US≡Britain/Bush≡Churchill — both coherent on relational grounds).

- **LISA model (Hummel & Holyoak 1997, 2003):** Symbolic-connectionist: semantic units → predicate/object units → proposition units. Role-filler bindings are encoded by **gamma-band (~40 Hz) temporal synchrony** — units within one proposition co-activate in phase; different propositions activate in anti-phase, enabling simultaneous but separated representations. WM is limited to 2–3 simultaneous propositions. Mapping connections grow whenever source and target units are co-active during retrieval. Accounts for frontal-damage analogical impairment and developmental relational shift.

- **Frontopolar cortex (BA-10) as multi-relational integration bottleneck:** BA-10 activates selectively when multiple relations must be **simultaneously integrated** (Christoff et al. 2001; Kroger et al. 2002; Cho et al. 2010) — not just for rule nesting depth. Bilateral middle/inferior frontal gyri handle WM maintenance of individual relations; inferior frontal gyrus handles interference suppression (semantically close distractors). Frontal lesions selectively impair only problems requiring multi-relation integration, not single-relation problems — a clean dissociation.

- **Schema induction as relational meta-learning:** Two compared analogs are sufficient to induce a reusable abstract schema. Progressive alignment (easy→hard: salient surface + relational match first, then relational match only) bootstraps schema formation from concrete episodes. Induced schemas retroactively increase retrieval of prior stored analogs and improve transfer to problems encountered before schema acquisition. This is the analogue-level mechanism for slow-W structure extraction from episodic comparison.

---

## Limitations

- All major computational models (LISA, SME, AMBR) require **hand-coded relational representations** as input — autonomous learning of predicates from non-relational perceptual inputs is largely unsolved, though Doumas et al. 2008 begin to address it.
- The four-stage pipeline is an oversimplification; real analogical reasoning is cyclic, interactive, and open-ended — analogs are re-represented *during* reasoning.
- Animal models are insufficient for human-level relational reasoning: there may be a qualitative evolutionary discontinuity in role-based relational reasoning (Penn, Holyoak & Povinelli 2008).
