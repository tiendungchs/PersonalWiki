# Penn, Holyoak & Povinelli (2008) — Darwin's Mistake

Penn, D. C., Holyoak, K. J., & Povinelli, D. J. (2008). *Darwin's mistake: Explaining the discontinuity between human and nonhuman minds.* Behavioral and Brain Sciences 31(2), 109–178. doi:10.1017/S0140525X08003543. (BBS target article + open peer commentary + authors' response.)

## Key computational insights

- **Relational Reinterpretation (RR) hypothesis** — a functional discontinuity, not continuity: only humans reinterpret first-order perceptual relations as higher-order, role-governed, explicitly structural relations (approximating a PSS). Full treatment: [[wiki/concepts/relational-reinterpretation.md]].
- **Chunking + segmentation** is the mechanism by which non-human minds pass relational tasks *without* relational reasoning: collapse a relation into an analog scalar (Shannon entropy of display variability), then apply conditional discrimination. Empirically grounded: 16-item same/different is *easier* than 2-item; baboon RMTS collapses on "different"-at-2-items (small entropy) but not "same" (zero entropy); Sarah-the-chimp's "analogies" tracked number of featural changes (Oden et al. 2001). The representational-level root of shortcut learning.
- **Diagnostic vocabulary:** feature-based vs. role-based (categories/rules/systematicity); functional (van Gelder: build+decompose) vs. concatenative (PSS: role-filler-preserving) compositionality; type/token separation. Non-human minds are "syntactically structured, functionally compositional, and featurally systematic" — but nothing more.
- **Degree→kind logic:** difference of degree in *how closely* PSS features are approximated (representational level) → difference of kind in *what reasoning is manifest* (functional level).
- **LISA as existence proof** that the higher-order relational system can be grafted onto a proto-symbolic conjunctive store via temporal-synchrony role-filler binding; the hard part is role-filler independence AND dynamic binding at once → argues against scale/size alone. Locus: PFC synchrony.

## Limitations

- Critics (Bermúdez) charge RR *redescribes* rather than explains the discontinuity — it does not say *why* the second system is human-only or *how* the graft interface works.
- Representational-level only: the physical/neural change is left unspecified (PFC synchrony is a suggestion, not a result).
- Comparative-behavioral evidence base (2008); pre-dates modern deep learning, so the "no non-classical net can do X" claims should be read against MLC / VSA results now in the wiki.
