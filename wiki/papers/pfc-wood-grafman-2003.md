# Human PFC: Processing and Representational Perspectives — Wood & Grafman 2003

**Citation:** Wood, J. N. & Grafman, J. (2003). Human prefrontal cortex: processing and representational perspectives. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 4(2), 139–147.

---

## Key Computational Insights

- **WM = activated long-term memory:** PFC neurons store persistent memory representations; working memory is those representations in an actively maintained state. "Processes" in cognition are representations that remain activated over time — resolves the process/representation debate in favor of representation and mechanistically grounds WM in sustained PFC firing.
- **SEC framework:** PFC stores Structured Event Complexes (SECs) — goal-oriented, temporally ordered event sequences encoding abstractions, rules, grammars, social schemas, and event boundaries. Activating an SEC generates predictions about subsequent events; PFC lesions disrupt this predictive function and impair day-to-day sequential behavior.
- **Anterior-posterior event-complexity gradient:** Anterior/frontopolar PFC = longer, multi-event SECs with slow activation and weak neighboring-event facilitation; posterior PFC = shorter SECs with strong inhibition of neighboring events. Left hemisphere: single-event processing; right: cross-temporal integration across multiple events. Convergent with Friedman & Robbins 2021 rule-nesting gradient (two descriptions of the same rostro-caudal axis).
- **vmPFC/dlPFC categorical specificity:** vmPFC (amygdala + HC connectivity) = social/emotional SECs; dlPFC (BG + premotor connectivity) = mechanistic/action SECs. Categorical specificity is determined by connectivity pattern to subcortical/posterior regions, not response strength balance.
- **Representational evaluation criterion:** A valid PFC theory must specify *what* is stored (memory features: age of acquisition, associative strength, activation frequency) to enable testable predictions about lesion effects. Pure processing accounts cannot meet this criterion.

---

## Limitations

- SEC predictions have mixed empirical support; vmPFC/dlPFC categorical dissociation confirmed broadly, but spatial specificity within categories is coarse.
- 2003 review paper: predates TMS-causal dissection (Friedman & Robbins 2021) and optogenetics; circuit mechanisms unspecified.
- Survey function: evaluates 8 PFC theories against 5 criteria but definitively confirms none.
