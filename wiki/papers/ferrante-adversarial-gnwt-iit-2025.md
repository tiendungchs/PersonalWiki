# Adversarial Testing of GNW and IIT Theories of Consciousness

**Citation:** Ferrante et al., *Nature* 2025. Preregistered adversarial collaboration (n=256) testing Integrated Information Theory (IIT) vs. Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) via fMRI (n=120), MEG (n=102), and iEEG (n=34) across multiple independent laboratories.

---

## Five Key Computational Insights

- **Offset ignition absent in PFC.** All 655 PFC electrodes fail to show the GNWT-predicted onset+offset activation pattern (BF₀₁ > 3 for all). Onset ignition IS observed, but the workspace update at the moment of conscious content transition (stimulus offset → blank screen) is absent from PFC. Offset responses are found in visual cortex, ruling out sensitivity issues. This is GNWT's central pre-registered failure: the mechanism by which the workspace *updates* at each conscious boundary is not observed in the predicted region.

- **PFC decodes coarse category, not fine-grained content.** Category (face/object) decodes from middle/inferior frontal cortex (~70% iEEG cross-task, ~60% fMRI searchlight). But orientation decoding fails in PFC across all three modalities (iEEG BF₀₁ = 5.11–8.65 supporting null; ~35% MEG signal attributed to possible leakage). Identity information is absent in PFC for all categories. Adding PFC to posterior ROIs does not improve decoding accuracy (face/object: iEEG BF₀₁ = 1.94×10⁴; orientation: iEEG BF₀₁ = 1,205). The GNW workspace broadcasts abstract categorical content, not perceptual detail.

- **Posterior cortex sustains fine-grained content (IIT prediction 2 partially passes).** Lateral fusiform gyrus maintains category- and identity-specific sustained responses that track stimulus duration (iEEG cross-temporal RSA matches IIT model, not GNWT). Only 15% (8/53) of face-selective electrodes sustain duration-tracking; the majority transiently activate at onset. Face orientation is not sustained beyond ~0.5 s, an IIT failure for that dimension.

- **No sustained gamma-band synchronization within posterior cortex (IIT prediction 3 fails).** The preregistered PPC (pairwise phase consistency) analysis finds no sustained gamma-band connectivity between V1/V2 and category-selective areas. Connectivity is low-frequency (2–25 Hz) and brief, mostly explained by the stimulus-evoked response (Bayesian null: BF₀₁ = 1.15–4.9). This is IIT's pre-registered critical failure: network connectivity does not sustain in the way that specifying consciousness requires.

- **Brief frontal-posterior gamma connectivity found (exploratory DFC, partial GNWT support).** A non-preregistered dynamic functional connectivity analysis (Gaussian copula mutual information) reveals content-selective gamma-band connectivity between PFC and category-selective areas within the GNWT-predicted 0.3–0.5 s window (iEEG DFC). This exploratory result partially supports GNWT's long-range connectivity prediction, contrasting with the preregistered PPC null result for connectivity.

---

## Limitations

- V1/V2 coverage sparse in iEEG (12 electrodes vs. 472 in PFC), limiting power for IIT's critical connectivity prediction in the most important region.
- Study tests *biological implementations* of theories, not their mathematical cores (IIT's Φ, GNWT's global workspace formalism) — theorists can retain the math while revising biological claims.
- Cannot fully exclude residual task effects for category-level (not orientation/identity) results, though task-irrelevant analyses were prioritized.

## Connections

- **[[wiki/entities/gwt-model.md]]** — offset ignition failure and content-specificity dissociation (category yes, orientation/identity no) are two new Contradictions extending the iES challenge from Raccah et al.; together they narrow GNW's explanatory scope from "consciousness" to "categorical access."
- **[[wiki/entities/prefrontal-cortex.md]]** — reinforces the constitutive/enabling distinction with correlational (decoding) evidence: PFC tracks conscious categorical content but not fine-grained perceptual dimensions, consistent with an enabling rather than constitutive role.
- **[[wiki/papers/raccah-pfc-consciousness-2021.md]]** — causal iES complement: Raccah shows local PFC disruption doesn't alter percepts; Ferrante shows PFC encoding is limited to categorical content — two orthogonal evidence types converging on the same conclusion.
- **[[wiki/papers/gnw-mashour-2020.md]]** — primary GNW review that predicted both offset ignition and full-content representation in PFC; Ferrante directly tests and finds partial failure for both.
- **[[wiki/concepts/working-memory.md]]** — the offset ignition failure raises the question of whether WM *release* from the global workspace requires frontal participation; Trübutschek's active/silent distinction (onset reinstates items) may need extension to account for offset transitions without frontal ignition.
