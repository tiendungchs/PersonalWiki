# A Natural History of the Human Mind — Sherwood, Subiaul & Zawidzki 2008

*J. Anat. 212(4):426–454. doi:10.1111/j.1469-7580.2008.00868.x.* Comparative reconstruction of the last common ancestor (LCA) of hominins/panins vs. modern humans across behavior, neuroanatomy, and genes.

## Key computational insights

- **Domain-general → domain-specific emergence (central thesis).** Human reasoning specializations are *not* new domain-specific modules — macaque homologues exist for ~all human cortical areas, and total frontal/prefrontal fraction is at ape-scaling predictions. Instead, quantitative reweighting of **domain-general primitives (attention, executive control, working memory, inhibition)**, amplified through an extended developmental window, yields emergent domain-specific skills. Architectural claim: *schedule + reweighting of general primitives*, not module addition.
- **Generalized systematicity via cross-domain connectivity ("common code").** Enhanced long-range corticocortical association fibers (arcuate fasciculus → middle-temporal semantic areas, more prominent in humans than chimp/macaque) integrate previously isolated modules → task-domain-neutral, systematically combinable thought → analogical transfer ("light as a wave").
- **Inhibitory "cognitive self-control" as latent-cause substrate.** Reasoning about unobservable causes requires *inhibiting stereotyped responses to superficially similar stimuli*, treating them differently when hidden causes differ — greater ape/human inhibitory control (DLPFC) is proposed as the enabling primitive.
- **Observable/unobservable decoupling (Povinelli).** An agent may reason about "eyes" without reasoning about "seeing" — binding observable correlates to latent causal variables is the crux; the see/not-see paradigms cannot isolate it.
- **Neoteny specifics:** human brain 27% adult size at birth (vs. 70% macaque, 36% chimp), fetal growth rate through year 1 (joint-attention onset inside this window); enhanced adult synaptic plasticity (THBS2/4, upregulated plasticity genes). The delayed-expression claim is directly measured by [[wiki/papers/somel-transcriptional-neoteny-2009.md]] (adult human PFC ≈ juvenile chimp; mosaic, gray-matter-specific, adolescence-peaked).
- **Hominid neuromodulatory upgrade:** denser 5-HT/DA/ACh innervation of prefrontal L5/6 + varicose axon "coils" (plasticity markers) vs. macaque.

## Limitations

Non-computational review; correlational structure-function links, small comparative samples (often 1–2 individuals/species for PFC volumetrics); the "model" is a verbal sketch, not a formal mechanism. Prefrontal-enlargement claims are contradictory across studies.
