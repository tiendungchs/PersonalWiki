# Crustacean Hemiellipsoid Bodies

**Paired neuropil structures in malacostracan crustaceans (crabs, lobsters, mantis shrimp) considered the structural sister to insect mushroom bodies — sharing the Kenyon-cell-like sparse expansion architecture, with preliminary evidence of place-cell-like activity in some species.**

> **Source caveat:** All claims below are from a brainstorming transcript. Treat as (tentative) throughout; primary literature verification required.

---

## Anatomy (tentative)

- Located in the deutocerebrum of malacostracan crustaceans.
- Contain Kenyon-cell-like neurons with sparse activation and multimodal input (visual, olfactory, mechanosensory) converging in a calyx-like input region.
- Project to output regions with convergent connectivity — the same expansion → compression ratio as insect mushroom bodies.
- Considered the **evolutionary sister structure** to insect mushroom bodies; both are thought to derive from a common ancestral structure in the last common ancestor of insects and crustaceans (~400–450 Mya).

---

## Behavioral / Physiological Evidence (tentative)

- (tentative) Electrophysiological recordings from crabs during spatial exploration show neurons with place-cell-like activity — location-specific firing that is maintained across movement trajectories, consistent with an allocentric spatial representation.
- Mantis shrimp (*Stomatopoda*) have hemiellipsoid bodies that are **disproportionately large** relative to their body size and to other crustaceans — potentially correlated with their documented spatial navigation ability and complex visual processing.
- Lobsters and crabs demonstrate long-distance homing navigation, suggesting allocentric spatial capabilities consistent with a world model.

---

## Key Unknowns

- Whether the place-cell-like activity represents true allocentric coding (remapping with context, environment-independent coordinate system) or simpler egocentric/sensory responses.
- Whether a path integration mechanism is present (no evidence reported in transcript).
- Relationship between hemiellipsoid body function and the crustacean central complex (if present); insect CX and mushroom bodies are distinct; crustacean equivalent is less characterized.

## Comparison with Insect Mushroom Bodies

| | Insect mushroom bodies | Crustacean hemiellipsoid bodies |
|---|---|---|
| **Evolutionary relationship** | Sister structures (common ancestor) | Sister structures (common ancestor) |
| **Circuit motif** | Kenyon cells → lobes → MBONs | Kenyon-like → output neurons |
| **Primary established function** | Olfactory associative learning | Less characterized; possibly spatial |
| **Spatial memory evidence** | Tentative (jumping spiders) | Tentative (crabs) |
| **Spatial scale** | Small brains; jumping spiders <500k neurons total | Crab/lobster brains larger; mantis shrimp notable for size |

---

## Limitations

- Weakest evidence tier in the convergent coding table: structural motif + one tentative place-cell report, no mechanistic confirmation.
- The "place-cell-like" claim requires replication and demonstration of allocentric (not retinotopic or egocentric) coding before it can be placed on equal footing with the HC or CX evidence.
