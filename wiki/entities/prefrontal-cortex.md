# Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)

Anterior cortex rostral to premotor areas. Divided into two functional streams with distinct connectivity and computational roles.

---

## Anatomy and Connectivity

| Subregion | Connectivity | Computational role |
|---|---|---|
| **vmPFC (BA-10/11/12)** | Amygdala, hippocampus, temporal visual areas | Goal value; social/emotional SECs; abstract grid codes (Constantinescu 2016) |
| *(medial/anterior BA-10 overlap)* | — | vmPFC's grid-code site (metric, continuous structural coding) and frontopolar's 4th-order rule-of-rules node (discrete policy hierarchy, Badre 2010) both sit in BA-10 but are not established as the same circuit — see [[wiki/queries/mec-abstract-codes-vs-declarative-rules.md]] for the open medial/lateral dissociation question |
| **dlPFC (BA-8/9/46)** | Basal ganglia, premotor cortex, parietal cortex | Goal maintenance; WM interference resistance; contextual rule keeping |
| **pmPFC (posterior medial PFC, ~area 8b)** | pre-SMA, premotor areas, rostral cingulate motor area | **Tactic selection**: which response *strategy* to apply when ≥2 strategies are simultaneously available; demand-responsive (silent under single-strategy conditions even when actions vary); distinct from ACC conflict monitoring |
| **Frontopolar (BA-10, anterior)** | Bilateral PFC, dorsal/ventral streams | **Simultaneous multi-relational integration** (Christoff 2001; Kroger 2002; Cho 2010): selectively activated when ≥2 relations must be integrated at once, not merely for rule nesting depth; also: branching subgoal management; longest-duration SECs |
| **ACC (BA-24/32)** | Brainstem neuromodulatory nuclei, SMA | Unsigned prediction error; proactive/reactive CC (Cognitive Control) modulation |
| **RIFG (BA-44/45)** | Subthalamic nucleus (hyperdirect pathway) | Response inhibition; stop-signal via STN (Subthalamic Nucleus) |

PFC pyramidal cells have more dendritic spines than other cortical pyramidal cells → broader multi-source integration. Majority of dlPFC delay-period neurons show transient (not persistent) activity.

**DNC synapse anatomy (Arnsten et al. 2010):** WM-maintaining recurrent connections in dlPFC L3 occur preferentially on **long, pedunculated spines with narrow necks** (high electrical resistance → effective shunting by ion channel opening). These are distinct from the short mushroom spines of LTP/LTD. The narrow neck is the morphological substrate for HCN/KCNQ channel shunting of network inputs. Network firing is tuned by GABAergic basket and chandelier cells (the same interneuron types disrupted in schizophrenia) providing lateral inhibition so that preferred-direction populations fire while nonpreferred are suppressed.

**Developmental trajectory of PFC rsFC:** PFC functional connectivity matures from predominantly local/proximal connections in early childhood (~4–5 years, mean PFC rsFC ≈ 0.57) to more distributed long-range architecture in adulthood (mean PFC rsFC ≈ 0.75); driven by myelination and synaptic pruning. Individual differences in adult PFC rsFC predict EF performance (Stroop r ≈ −0.45; higher connectivity → faster incongruent RT); the same link holds in children (Day-Night accuracy r ≈ 0.70). The local→distributed shift is the structural correlate of EF emergence (Fair et al. 2008/2009; Eng et al. 2025).

## Functional Properties

- **Tactic selection and sensorimotor cascade (pmPFC, Matsuzaka et al. 2012; Awan et al. 2020)** — pmPFC neurons (~area 8b, rostral to pre-SMA) encode *which response tactic* to apply, not which specific action and not whether conflict exists. Key signatures (2012): (1) 51% of pmPFC neurons are tactic-selective in mixed-tactic conditions; (2) activity nearly vanishes when only a single tactic is needed, even when actions continue to vary; (3) neuronal ensemble discriminates tactic ~125–145ms before the motor response (247–313ms RT), confirming the representation is predictive not reactive; (4) ACC showed only 5/260 concordance-selective neurons, confirming tactic selection and conflict monitoring are computationally distinct. When single-tactic training installs a habit (cortico-BG loop), pmPFC disengages and reaction times decrease — supervisory tactic control is bypassed by automatization. **Three-area cascade (2020):** CPD analysis reveals a full abstraction gradient — pmPFC encodes tactics + cue position + action (all three); pre-SMA encodes tactics + action (no spatial); SMA encodes mainly action. **Spatial gating:** pre-SMA receives abundant PFC afferents but sparse direct parietal input; pmPFC involvement in multi-tactic conditions relieves pre-SMA from processing cue location directly (consistent with prior single-tactic studies that found pre-SMA spatial tuning when pmPFC was disengaged). **Schizophrenia validation:** PFC hypoactivity → compensatory pre-SMA overactivation (Cieslik et al. 2015), confirming pmPFC normally suppresses downstream motor areas rather than merely exciting them.
- **Self-referential simulation / default-state function (dorsal MPFC BA 8/9/10)** — medial extensions of BA 8/9/10 specifically increase during self-referential/introspective tasks and correlate with SIT (stimulus-independent thought) count at rest; this is the seat of daydreams and inner monologue (Gusnard et al. 2001). Ingvar's interpretation (via Gusnard 2001): this activity is continuous inner rehearsal and optimization of behavioral programs — maintaining a narrative/autobiographical self with integrated access to past, present, and anticipated future. This default-state function is suppressed when externally directed CC tasks engage the system. Ventral MPFC (BA 24/25/32) by contrast performs ongoing emotional/salience monitoring that decreases under any attentional load regardless of task content.
- **Goal maintenance** — PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) goal representations send top-down bias signals enabling non-prepotent task-correct responses to win lateral inhibition competition in posterior cortex (Miller & Cohen 2001)
- **SEC storage** — stores Structured Event Complexes: goal-oriented temporal event sequences encoding rules, abstractions, grammars (Wood & Grafman 2003); SEC (Structured Event Complex) activation predicts subsequent events
- **Hierarchical gradient** — rostro-caudal: BA (Brodmann Area)-10/FPC (long SECs / 4th-order rules-of-rules) → BA (Brodmann Area)-9/46/mid-DLPFC (contextual rule / WM hub / 3rd-order) → BA (Brodmann Area)-6/44/prePMd (2nd-order policy: context → relevant rule set) → BA (Brodmann Area)-6/PMd (1st-order: stimulus → response); mid-lateral is the integration hub; the hierarchy extends into premotor cortex, not just PFC proper
- **RL-based abstract rule discovery** — the rostro-caudal hierarchy supports not only execution of known rules but discovery of abstract rules via RL without explicit instruction (Badre et al. 2010). prePMd (~BA6/44) activates from trial 1 of learning — before any 2nd-order rule is known — as the search engine for higher-order policy abstraction. Early prePMd activation (collapsed across conditions) predicts subsequent 2nd-order rule discovery (r=0.51–0.56, p<0.05); early PMd activation does not (rs<0.3). prePMd activity is reward-gated: declines to baseline when no higher-order rule exists (Flat condition). Behavioral signature of success: step-wise sigmoid learning curves (large α, small β) vs. gradual curves for 1st-order rote learning. The same rostro-caudal hierarchy handles RL-based discovery and explicit rule execution via the same regions at the same MNI coordinates
- **Meta-RL** — Dopamine encodes slow synaptic updates (outer loop); PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) LSTM dynamics implement within-episode model-free RL (fast inner loop); model-based behavior emerges (Wang et al. 2018)
- **Multi-relational integration (BA-10 specificity)** — frontopolar activation scales with number of relations held simultaneously, not with rule nesting depth per se; inferior frontal gyrus (BA-44/45) handles distractor suppression (semantically close competitors) independently; together they implement the mapping constraint-satisfaction stage of analogical reasoning (Holyoak 2012)
- **WM** — activated long-term memory (Goldman-Rakic); dlPFC mediates interference resistance and updating specifically; transient delay activity is TRNN-compatible
- **PFdl attentional dominance (Lebedev et al. 2004 [[wiki/papers/lebedev-pfdl-attention-memory-2004.md]])** — single-neuron recordings in monkeys performing a task that independently controlled spatial attention and spatial memory simultaneously: 61% of spatially tuned PFdl neurons encoded the *attended* location, 16% the *remembered* location, 23% both (hybrid). The attended representation grew stronger as the trigger signal approached, ruling out a purely sensory account. Regional specialization: ventrolateral PFdl is attention-dominant (attention 28%, memory 2%); dorsomedial PFdl is mixed (8%/9%). Hybrid cells resolve ambiguity by encoding both variables with dissimilar spatial preferences. After saccade, the representation transitions smoothly from attended→newly fixated location — PFdl tracks behavioral relevance dynamically. **Design implication:** the dlPFC hub module should be conceptualized as an attentional selector that secondarily encodes memory, not the reverse; Goldman-Rakic's maintenance-memory theory fails to account for the majority of PFdl delay activity.
- **Schema formation and expression** — mPFC (especially vmPFC) reconciles overlapping associations during learning (detects conflict when A-B and A-C share element A); creates schematic organizations during consolidation via sustained vmPFC-HC coupling; selects the correct schema during expression by sending context-appropriate bias to dorsal HC via perirhinal/LEC. Lateral PFC emphasizes distinctiveness of memories rather than integration. vmPFC-HC coupling during overlapping-pair learning predicts subsequent transitive inference performance (Preston & Eichenbaum 2013). Maps to SEC (Structured Event Complex) storage (Wood & Grafman 2003): schemas are organized temporal event sequences stored in vmPFC/mPFC.
- **vmPFC→MEC memory-organization control (De Sousa et al. 2026)** — vmPFC exerts direct top-down control over HC memory organization through excitatory projections (deep-layer, GAD67−) to MEC layers II/III/V, which then modulate NGF/NDNF+ inhibitory cells in the CA1 SLM. vmPFC activity is *higher* when memories should be kept separate (different contexts, 7 days apart) and *lower* when integration is appropriate (same context, or 5h interval). Inhibiting vmPFC→MEC terminals during encoding of a second memory forces integration even at 7 days; activating them during same-context revisit forces separation. This is a bidirectional memory-organization switch operated by consolidated vmPFC representations. The circuit is time-gated: systems consolidation (~7 days) must first establish the vmPFC representation of the prior memory; at 5h, the circuit is not yet recruited. Key implication: vmPFC is not merely a schema *reader* (selecting schemas at retrieval) but also a schema *enforcer* at encoding — actively preventing inappropriate integration of unrelated memories by controlling which neurons HC uses to encode new events. See [[wiki/papers/desousa-pfc-memory-organization-hpc-2026.md]] and [[wiki/entities/hippocampal-entorhinal-system.md]] (vmPFC→MEC section).
- **NREM slow-wave generation and consolidation (mPFC as SWA source, Mander et al. 2013 [[wiki/papers/prefrontal-atrophy-nrem-mander-2013.md]])** — mPFC is the dominant EEG source generator of NREM slow-wave activity (SWA: 0.8–4.6 Hz); mPFC grey matter volume predicts global SWA (r = 0.89, p < 0.001); other atrophy-prone regions (HC, temporal lobe, precuneus) do not mediate age effects on SWA — the relationship is regionally specific. Age-related mPFC atrophy mediates reduced SWA (Sobel p = 0.005), which fully mediates impaired overnight memory retention (p < 0.001). **Functional consequence:** sufficient SWA → post-sleep hippocampal activation decreases (HC-independent retrieval) + HC-vmPFC functional connectivity increases = successful hippocampal-neocortical memory transformation. Insufficient SWA → persistent HC dependence during retrieval and reduced HC-mPFC coupling = failed schema consolidation. **Architectural implication:** mPFC is not a passive schema organizer; it actively generates the oscillatory signal (SWA) that drives its own offline storage process — a feedback loop in which the schema hub creates the consolidation scaffold for its own content.
- **HC-mPFC theta coupling as retrieval mechanism (Euston et al. 2012 [[wiki/papers/euston-mpfc-memory-decision-2012.md]])** — ~50% of mPFC cells phase-lock to hippocampal theta (4–8 Hz) during spatial tasks. Theta coherence (spike-LFP and LFP-LFP) increases as the animal approaches memory-guided choice points, and increases further after acquisition of a new rule. Reduced phase-locking *predicts errors* — synchrony is either necessary for correct retrieval or reflects decision confidence. HC-mPFC functional disconnection (unilateral mPFC + contralateral HC inactivation) ≈ bilateral mPFC inactivation across water maze, T-maze, radial arm, and Hebb-Williams tasks — confirming that mPFC depends entirely on the unidirectional HC→mPFC pathway for both context provision and rapid associative learning.
- **Conflict-evoked theta gating** — during conflict tasks, non-phase-locked theta (4–8 Hz) power increases in VLPFC (anterior IFG) and mPFC; this induced theta synchronizes frontal ensembles and gates downstream response selection; causal evidence via VCVS DBS (Widge et al. 2019); resting-state theta is unchanged — the mechanism is conflict-specific, not tonic; VLPFC theta change predicts clinical CC improvement (r=0.76, AUC=0.87)
- **Spacetime Attractor: mechanistic circuit theory of PFC planning (Jensen et al. 2026 [[wiki/papers/mechanistic-planning-pfc-jensen-2026.md]])** — proposes that PFC contains a spacetime attractor (STA): distinct neural subspaces (indexed by delay δ = 0…T−1) simultaneously encode the expected agent location at each future time step. Inter-subspace connectivity encodes the environment adjacency matrix; attractor dynamics infer the optimal trajectory from current location and reward inputs. Key findings: (i) STA is the only tested algorithm that handles within-trial dynamic rewards (the class of problems PFC is known to be critical for); (ii) RNNs meta-trained on dynamic planning tasks spontaneously learn STA-like dynamics — confirmed by recurrent weight analysis (r=0.91 ± 0.07 to adjacency matrix), explicit future-state decoding, and perturbation/attractor signature tests; (iii) conveyor belt dynamics (δ=k shifts to δ=k−1 after each action) directly match El-Gaby et al. 2023 PFC sequence WM recordings; (iv) this unifies PFC sequence WM and planning under a single mechanistic framework. Experimental predictions: optogenetic activation of a future subspace should bias behaviour toward the stimulated state at the corresponding delay; effective connectivity between subspaces should reflect environment structure. See [[wiki/entities/spacetime-attractor.md]] and [[wiki/concepts/planning-as-inference.md]].
- **Topological map learning and spreading activation planning (Martinet et al. 2011 [[wiki/papers/spatial-learning-pfc-martinet-2011.md]])** — a recurrent network of cortical minicolumns learns a sparse topological graph from redundant hippocampal place-cell inputs via unsupervised Hebbian learning: each column → one environmental location (node); collateral plasticity encodes adjacency (edges). Planning uses spreading activation in two phases: (1) reward signal back-propagates from the goal column through reverse associations (ψ connections), decaying exponentially per synapse — firing rate thereby encodes distance-to-goal; (2) once the current-position column detects coincident location + goal signals, a forward path signal propagates (φ connections) toward the goal; WTA among minicolumn neurons selects the optimal action. A second-level β column population uses a proprioceptive turning-probability signal to encode corridor-level topology at coarser resolution, enabling planning to scale to 4× larger environments. Four functionally distinct PFC neuron types confirmed against electrophysiological recordings: (i) location-selective (symmetric RF, high spatial info); (ii) distance-to-goal (unique preferred firing frequency inversely correlated to distance); (iii) prospective (asymmetric left-skewed tuning, anticipatory discharge ~75 ms ahead); (iv) state-action value (WTA action selection, tracks reward contingency switches). **Key HC/PFC distinction:** HC provides redundant distributed place code for localization; PFC compresses it to compact topological graph for planning — unidirectional input, not equivalence.
- **Grid-gated decision-value construction in mPFC (Park, Miller & Boorman 2021 [[wiki/papers/park-social-hierarchy-grid-code-2021.md]])** — during inference over a reconstructed 2D social hierarchy, mPFC (with EC and TPJ) shows 6-fold hexagonal modulation for *inferred* direct trajectories, and encodes the decision value (pair growth-potential, GP) — with the two effects *independent* (near-zero regressor correlation, both survive in one GLM). Critically, **GP value coding is stronger for grid-aligned than misaligned trajectories** (6-fold specific), and subjects with stronger EC grid modulation show stronger GP coding in TPJ. The value-comparison term |GP1−GP2| appears in vmPFC + EC/HC. Interpretation: the entorhinal grid frame is theta-coupled to value coding in mPFC/TPJ, so **decision values are constructed *on* the abstract map** — mPFC is where the structural (grid) code is read out into choice value, not merely maintained. Complements the vmPFC abstract-grid site (Constantinescu 2016) with a functional value-usage link.
- **Abstract variable geometry** — pre-stimulus, DLPFC and ACC (Anterior Cingulate Cortex) represent the hidden context variable in abstract format (high CCGP (Cross-Condition Generalization Performance): coding direction parallel across condition subsets); during the decision epoch, DLPFC loses context abstraction as context is non-linearly combined with stimulus identity for action selection (CCGP drops to chance, traditional decoding unchanged); value and action achieve high CCGP (Cross-Condition Generalization Performance) in DLPFC during the decision; CCGP (Cross-Condition Generalization Performance) for context correlates with behavioral accuracy on error trials (Bernardi et al. 2020)

## mPFC Projection-Specific Encoding

mPFC excitatory neurons in the prelimbic cortex are functionally specialized by output target (Lai et al. 2026 [[wiki/papers/lai-mpfc-bla-nac-affective-2026.md]]). The same cortical region contains interleaved populations with divergent affective tuning:

| Pathway | Valence tuning | center-ON neuron density | Social stimulus encoding |
|---|---|---|---|
| **mPFC→BLA** | Negatively valenced: anxiety-like states; center zone, open arms, aversive cues | Higher proportion | Population level: better separates social vs. non-social |
| **mPFC→NAc** | Positively valenced: exploration (sniffing), social preference | Lower proportion; center-ON neurons also activate during sniffing | center-ON subpopulation level: better separates social vs. non-social (via pattern decorrelation) |

**Key findings:**
- Population-level Ca²⁺ transient rates are statistically equivalent across pathways — functional specialization is invisible at the rate-coding level; it requires ensemble PCA trajectories or subpopulation analysis.
- mPFC→NAc encodes social preference via *pattern decorrelation* (reduced pairwise Pearson correlation, measured as FWHM of correlation distribution); mPFC→BLA does not. The encoding is in ensemble co-activity structure, not firing rates.
- The two pathways provide complementary, scale-dependent encoding: mPFC→BLA is the generalized aversion-processing channel at the population level; mPFC→NAc's center-ON subpopulation is the targeted social/exploratory preference channel.
- Chronic social subordination (tube test loser) elevates corticosterone, heightens anxiety, and abolishes social preference — independently validating that these behavioral proxies reflect genuine internal affective states.

**Architectural implication:** Projection identity is part of a cortical neuron's functional identity. A model cannot treat all mPFC outputs as interchangeable; the output target determines what is encoded. Valence routing (aversive → BLA; appetitive → NAc) is a structural computational principle in mPFC — instantiating dual-stream processing within a single cortical region via projection specificity. See [[wiki/concepts/pattern-separation.md]] for the ensemble decorrelation mechanism and [[wiki/concepts/neuromodulation.md]] for projection-specific functional diversity.

## iES Evidence: Subregion Dissociation for Consciousness

Raccah, Block & Fox (J Neurosci 2021) [[wiki/papers/raccah-pfc-consciousness-2021.md]] synthesize intracranial electrical stimulation (iES) data from 67+ patients and 1537+ electrode sites to test whether PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) *constitutes* conscious experience.

| Subregion | iES elicitation rate | Effect type |
|---|---|---|
| Lateral/anterolateral PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) (dlPFC, frontopolar BA (Brodmann Area)-10) | Near 0% — lowest in the brain | None reliably; occasional isolated reports of conceptual thoughts (not percept changes) |
| OFC (Orbitofrontal Cortex) (posterior > anterior; anterior BA (Brodmann Area)-10 completely silent) | ~17% overall | Olfactory, gustatory, somatosensory, and emotional experiences; intensity scales with mA |
| ACC (Anterior Cingulate Cortex) (sgACC/pgACC/aMCC) | Moderate | Emotional regulation, motivational feelings (perseverance), visceral, fear |
| Unimodal sensory cortex | Up to ~67% | Content-specific perceptual changes (phosphenes, face distortions, etc.) |

**Key distinction — constitutive vs. enabling:** PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) enables consciousness (via arousal, attention, WM) without constituting it. iES can disrupt enabling factors without changing percepts; disrupting a constitutive region changes percepts. Lateral PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) disruption leaves percepts intact → enabling role, not constitutive.

**Dense-code explanation:** PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) neurons show mixed-selectivity, non-topographic codes distributed across large circuits. Disrupting a small iES patch leaves the population representation intact. This predicts low elicitation rate without refuting PFC's computational role — it is a consequence of the CCGP (Cross-Condition Generalization Performance)/SD geometry in which representations are invariant to local perturbation.

This evidence does not challenge PFC's role in CC, WM, reasoning, or meta-learning — all of which involve PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) *enabling* processing, not constituting phenomenal experience. It specifically challenges GNW's claim that lateral PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) hub ignition is the neural substrate of conscious access.

## Decoding Evidence: PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) Encodes Category, Not Fine-Grained Content

Ferrante et al. (Nature 2025) [[wiki/papers/ferrante-adversarial-gnwt-iit-2025.md]] provide correlational (multi-modal decoding) evidence complementing the causal iES evidence from Raccah et al., converging on the same conclusion via a different method.

| Content dimension | PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) decoding | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Face/object category | Yes — ~70% iEEG, ~60% fMRI searchlight | Middle/inferior frontal cortex; cross-task generalizable |
| Orientation (left/right/front) | No | iEEG BF₀₁ = 5.11–8.65 supporting null across all modalities |
| Stimulus identity (20 exemplars) | No | Absent in PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) for all categories (iEEG RSA) |
| Adding PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) to posterior ROIs | No improvement | face/object iEEG BF₀₁ = 1.94×10⁴; orientation iEEG BF₀₁ = 1,205 |

**Implication:** PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) does not represent the full phenomenal richness of conscious experience. Its access-consciousness role is bounded to coarse categorical distinctions; fine-grained perceptual content (the specific face, its orientation) is sustained in posterior cortex (lateral fusiform gyrus), not in the frontal workspace. This is consistent with PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) representing abstract goal-relevant categories (SEC framework, Wood & Grafman 2003) rather than sensory instances.

**Design implication:** In a reasoning model, the PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) hub module should receive input at the *abstract/categorical* representation level, not at the raw feature level. Downstream fine-grained retrieval requires querying the posterior maintenance store, not the hub itself.

## Single-Neuron Category and Rule Encoding (Miller, Freedman & Wallis 2002)

The empirical foundation for PFC as the store of abstract task knowledge — single-unit evidence complementing the Miller & Cohen (2001) cognitive-control model. See [[wiki/papers/pfc-categories-concepts-miller-2002.md]].

| Finding | Task | Key result | Design relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Category tuning in single neurons** | Cat/dog delayed match-to-category (DMC), 3-D morph continuum | Lateral PFC neurons treat 60/40 dog-like cats like prototype cats — *sharp-boundary* categorical tuning, not gradual similarity. Population tuning shifts toward category over stimulus identity | A discretization mechanism: continuous input → sharp-boundary symbol at the single-unit level |
| **Learned, remappable categories** | Retrain on 3 orthogonal boundaries | Neural activity shifts to the *new* (not old) category scheme after retraining | Categories are written into tuning by experience, not innate; the symbol alphabet is learnable and re-partitionable |
| **Abstract rules generalize to novel stimuli** | Match vs. non-match (cue modality dissociated from rule) | Rule-selective neurons independent of cue, remembered picture, response, reward; above-chance on *first-ever-seen* stimuli | Rule abstraction = **compression**: 2 rules replace 16 paired associates → learning shortcut (see [[wiki/concepts/abstract-reasoning.md]]) |
| **Nonlinear conjunctive tuning** | Object → saccade associative learning (Asaad et al. 1998) | ~44–50% of lateral PFC neurons conjunctively code object×saccade, not predictable from single factors; rule-specific baseline shifts flag active task | The empirical origin of **mixed selectivity** (see [[wiki/concepts/representational-geometry.md]]) |
| **Rapid plasticity** | Conditional object–saccade learning | Associations learned in 5–15 trials; response-related delay activity migrates earlier in time (toward cue) across trials; FEF acquires target-feature selectivity within minutes | Fast within-session credit assignment; activity-timing shift is a behavioral signature of learned prediction |

Category information may be *retrieved* from long-term ITC storage into PFC for immediate task use (Tomita et al. 1999: top-down PFC→ITC signals activate stored visual memories); the relative ITC vs. PFC contribution to category *storage* remains unresolved.

## Comparison with Hippocampal-Entorhinal System

| Feature | PFC (Prefrontal Cortex) | HC/MEC |
|---|---|---|
| Timescale | Seconds (WM); lifetime (SEC representations) | Fast binding (minutes); sleep-phase consolidation |
| Representation | SECs: temporal sequences, rules, grammars | Maps: g/x/p factorized; episodic (g, x) bindings |
| Generalization | Rule/grammar abstraction; cross-context transfer | Structural: same W across environments |

| ML analog | LSTM meta-RL + TRNN (Transition RNN) + three-level hierarchy | TEM W/M two-timescale factorized model |
