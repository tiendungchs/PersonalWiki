# Epistemic Audit of the Central Framing

**Question:** The wiki centers on the claim that *the brain's spatial-memory and cognitive-control machinery is re-recruited for abstract, non-spatial reasoning* — that reasoning reduces to **latent graph discovery** (infer a hidden graph, then navigate it). Is this an evidence-based conclusion, or an **anchoring artifact** of ingesting TEM first as the scaffold? In particular: might abstract path integration ("System 1: Navigator") be hosted **elsewhere** rather than generalized by the Hippocampal Formation?

---

## Verdict

The claim is **not one claim but three nested sub-claims of very different evidential strength.** TEM-anchoring has bent the *phrasing* and the *center of gravity* substantially, but has **not** manufactured the core empirical fact. The user's alternative hypothesis ("hosted elsewhere") is **partly confirmed by the wiki's own evidence.** One residual bias survives, and it is exactly where the frame is extended past its biological warrant.

| # | Sub-claim | Evidence strength | Anchoring-shaped? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | A **grid/metric code** generalizes beyond physical space to *continuous/orderable* abstract dimensions | **Strong, model-independent** (human fMRI + convergent evolution) | ❌ No |
| **B** | The **Hippocampal Formation specifically** is the host that generalizes this to reasoning | **Weak / partly contradicted** (abstract grid signal is strongest in vmPFC, not EC; TBT makes HC a prototype not a host) | ✅ Yes |
| **C** | **Abstract reasoning as a whole** (incl. discrete/declarative/symbolic) reduces to navigation over a latent graph | **A framing bet, not a finding** | ✅ Strongly |

*This table is the verdict **at audit open**. All 12 planned sources are now ingested; for the settled state see [**Terminal re-score — the audit closed**](#terminal-re-score--the-audit-closed-all-12-sources-ingested) below.*

## The anchoring audit — what is model-independent vs. TEM-shaped

**Model-independent (survives any ingestion order):**
- **Constantinescu et al. 2016** — hexagonal (cos 6θ) fMRI signature during navigation of an abstract non-spatial 2D "bird-morphology" space; replicated for social rank and odor space. Human neuroimaging, no model dependency.
- **Convergent evolution** — 5–6 independent lineages derived allocentric coding circuits (`convergent-allocentric-coding`). Independent of TEM.
- **The disconfirming evidence was actively ingested** — Kumaran & Maguire 2005 (declarative graph → zero MTL activation) and Badre 2010 (PFC rule hierarchy, "nothing resembling path integration") are logged as tensions and drove the two-system split. This is the *opposite* of confirmation-bias suppression, and it is the strongest reason the wiki is not merely an echo of its first ingest.

**TEM-shaped (would likely differ under a different scaffold-first ingest):**
- The **master reduction itself** — "reasoning = navigation over a latent graph" — is downstream of the navigation frame. Competing reductions (program induction / language-of-thought; active inference / free-energy; production systems) would de-center navigation and were never given an equal first-class hearing; the wiki folds them *into* LGD rather than treating LGD as one contestable frame.
- **System 2's vocabulary.** Even the declarative subsystem — which the biology says is *non-navigational* — is described as "path integration over an operator graph," a "Strategist" that asks "where do I want to go." The navigation metaphor is imported *past the boundary* (Kumaran & Maguire's zero-MTL result) where its biology holds.
- The **entity/block scaffolding** (Navigator/Strategist = MEC/PFC, `g/x` factorization, Transformation Inferrer as inverse path integration) is TEM's ontology throughout.

## Where the "hosted elsewhere" hypothesis is *correct*

The wiki's own evidence already answers the user's technical worry affirmatively for **sub-claim B**:
- Constantinescu's strongest abstract grid signal is in **vmPFC (Z=4.09), not entorhinal cortex.** The abstract grid *code* is most strongly **prefrontal/DMN**; HC/MEC is where it was *characterized for physical space*, not where it lives for abstract tasks.
- **TBT / Thousand-Brains** makes grid-like L6 coding potentially universal across cortex → HC is the **prototype, not the host** (`allocentric-navigation-direction-evaluation` already reframes the direction this way).
- The **declarative slice** (Kumaran & Maguire, Badre) is hosted in a PFC rostro-caudal RL hierarchy with no periodicity and no path integration — a genuinely different system.

**Honest restatement of B:** *the grid code is a domain-general primitive that recurs wherever the computational problem (continuous-coordinate assignment + cross-instance graph-matching) arises; its abstract locus is prefrontal, with HC as the metric-navigation prototype.* `mec-abstract-codes-vs-declarative-rules` already states this better than §5.0 of the SOTA review does.

---

## The residual bias (sub-claim C) — and how to resolve it

The frame is **validated exactly on the metric-continuous slice** and has a **documented negative result on the discrete-declarative slice** (Kumaran & Maguire) — which is arguably where the hardest part of ARC/math reasoning lives (Penn's System 2, role-filler binding, recursion). No one has shown a grid/path-integration code for a genuinely **non-metric, discrete-symbolic** inference (modular arithmetic, type-checking, syntactic recursion). Its absence is currently predicted equally by "reasoning isn't navigation" and by "we haven't looked" — **not yet diagnostic.**

**Discriminating design.** The frame (C) predicts map/grid-like codes for *any* relational reasoning. The falsifiers predict they should be *absent* for genuinely non-metric symbolic reasoning, or that a non-navigation frame (program induction / LoT / active inference) explains the same data more parsimoniously. Converting C from bet → finding (or falsifying it) requires ingesting evidence on **four axes** (sources below).

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-1 #1 (Garvert et al. 2017)

Garvert is the highest-value falsification target because it removes the two escape hatches that let sub-claim B/C stay comfortable: it is **discrete** (not continuous), **non-spatial**, has **no continuous organizing dimension** ("dimensions had to be extracted from the associations"), and is **unconscious/implicit**. A map-like code was nonetheless reconstructed from **entorhinal/subicular** fMRI adaptation, and both the neural signal and behavioral RT were best explained by **communicability / the successor representation** (weighted sum of future states), beating Euclidean distance in direct competition. Effect on each sub-claim:

| # | Prior verdict | Effect of Garvert | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong (grid/metric code → continuous/orderable abstract dims) | Mildly broadened: the entorhinal relational code is not confined to *continuous* structure — it also organizes a *discrete, transition-sampled* graph. (Garvert did not test hexagonal periodicity, so this speaks to the map, not grid-symmetry specifically.) | **Strong, slightly broadened** |
| **B** | Weak/partly contradicted (abstract grid strongest in vmPFC, not EC → HC is prototype not host) | **Partially reversed / made task-dependent.** For a *passive, implicitly-learned* relational graph the map is in **EC specifically and absent from vmPFC/OFC** — the opposite localization from Constantinescu's *active* task. So the host is not uniformly prefrontal: **EC hosts implicit/statistically-acquired relational maps; vmPFC/DMN is recruited when the structure is used for an active decision.** HC/EC is a genuine host, not merely a physical-space prototype. | **Context-dependent, not simply weak** |
| **C** | A framing bet | **Pushed rightward but not converted.** The frame is now confirmed on a genuinely *discrete, non-spatial, non-metric, unconscious* relational graph — and, tellingly, the recovered code is *literally the navigation object* (SR), the frame's own prediction. But the graph was small and **2D-planar-embeddable** (MDS recovered it) and learned **passively** — so this validates the *transition-sampled, low-dimensional* slice, not genuinely **non-embeddable discrete-symbolic** inference (modular arithmetic, syntactic recursion, type-checking), where C remains a bet. | **Bet → finding on the transition-sampled slice; still a bet on the symbolic slice** |

**Net effect on the frame.** The load-bearing boundary moved: the wiki's old "metric vs. declarative" fork is refined to **"acquired via experienced transitions (→ HC/EC map, SR metric) vs. known declaratively without traversal (→ mPFC/social-brain)."** Metric continuity is *sufficient but not necessary*; transition-experience is the deeper entry condition. This **narrows the Kumaran & Maguire negative** from "discrete graphs don't recruit HC" to "declaratively-known graphs don't" — a smaller residual for C to explain. What Garvert does **not** do: it does not touch the genuinely non-metric symbolic slice (Axis-1 #2–4 and Axis-2/3 still needed), and its passive design leaves the *active declarative reasoning* case (ARC/math System 2) open. **C is stronger than at audit time, but the remaining open region is exactly the hardest and most reasoning-relevant one.**

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-3 #8 (Chen et al. 2022)

Chen et al. is the mirror image of Garvert: not a *falsifier* but a **deflation** that comes from *inside* the cognitive-map camp — an opinion piece that pushes grid coding to its maximal extent (universal across S1, V2, auditory, frontal cortex) and, in doing so, exposes why "grid signal present during abstract task" is weaker evidence for the frame than the wiki has been treating it. It cuts sub-claims A and C in opposite directions to Garvert.

| # | Prior verdict | Effect of Chen 2022 | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong, slightly broadened (grid/metric code → continuous/orderable + transition-sampled abstract structure) | **Tempered on the human-abstract slice.** Chen concedes the abstract-domain grid evidence is (i) **BOLD-only, never single-unit-confirmed in humans**, (ii) prone to **false positives** ("detecting grid responses… relatively arbitrary threshold phenomena"; spatial-autocorrelation artifacts), and (iii) detectable only under **contrived ≈2D task designs** ("the lack of structure in tasks brings a difficulty of detecting generalized grid codes"). The animal single-unit evidence stays strong; the *human abstract* evidence is downgraded from "strong, model-independent" to "suggestive pending single-unit + non-contrived confirmation." | **Strong for physical/animal; suggestive-not-strong for human abstract** |
| **B** | Context-dependent (EC hosts implicit maps; vmPFC/DMN hosts active decisions) | **Broadened toward "no privileged host."** Grid cells are reported in rat S1 and V2 and predicted in auditory + frontal cortex; TBT + Chen make grid-like coding a candidate **universal cortical primitive**. This *supports* the audit's "hosted elsewhere" reframe (HC = prototype, not sole host) but pushes it further: the host may be *everywhere*, which is itself a problem for using localization to argue the frame. | **Distributed / no privileged host — "hosted elsewhere" confirmed to the point of dissolving the question** |
| **C** | Bet → finding on transition-sampled slice; still a bet on the symbolic slice | **Deflated as evidence, unchanged as hypothesis.** If grid geometry emerges **generically** from *any* SR-eigenvector or RNN-attractor computation over structured input, then observing a grid signature during an abstract task is **necessary-but-not-diagnostic**: it would appear during *any* recurrent computation with metric structure, whether or not the brain is "navigating" to reason. This does not falsify C, but it removes grid-presence from C's evidence column — the frame now needs a **positive test that grid geometry is *used* for the inference**, not merely present. | **Bet, with its cheapest supporting evidence (grid-presence) demoted to non-diagnostic** |

**Net effect on the frame.** Garvert pushed the B/C boundary rightward by adding a genuinely discrete non-metric graph; Chen pulls back on the *inferential weight* of the imaging evidence that made A look "strong." Together they sharpen the missing caveat the audit demanded: the frame is **best-supported where there is single-unit, non-contrived, transition-sampled evidence** (rodent physical space; Garvert's implicit graph) and **weakest exactly where the wiki leans hardest** (human abstract-2D fMRI, treated as load-bearing for A). The actionable upgrade is a **usage test**: a grid code counts as evidence for C only if perturbing/reading its geometry changes inference behavior — mere co-occurrence is a ubiquity artifact. See [[wiki/empirical-tensions.md]] (grid-ubiquity/detection deflation row).

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-1 #3 (Qu et al. 2026)

Qu et al. is the **positive-usage counterweight to Chen** and the developmental/individual-differences extension of Constantinescu+Garvert. N=203, ages 8–25, active 2D non-spatial inference task ([[wiki/papers/qu-grid-code-development-intelligence-2026.md]]). It is the first source in the audit to supply something approaching the **usage test** Chen demanded — not perturbation, but *dose-dependent behavioral necessity*: grid-code strength (i) **mediates** the age→inference gain, and (ii) specifically gates the trials that *cannot* be solved by a heuristic (incongruent 2D trials), while leaving heuristic-solvable congruent trials untouched. And it is the first to tie the code to **standardized fluid intelligence** (Raven's SPM).

| # | Prior verdict (post-Chen) | Effect of Qu 2026 | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong for physical/animal; suggestive-not-strong for human abstract (BOLD-only, contrived, false-positive-prone) | **Reinforced on the orderable-continuous slice, but does not escape Chen's core caveat.** Still BOLD-only and still a *contrived ≈2D* design — but now with N=203, three convergent detection methods, 6-fold specificity against 4/5/7/8-fold, and SNR/SES/training controls. This is the most robust *human-abstract* grid measurement in the wiki, though the dimensions remain **orderable/metric** (5 ranks/dim), so it broadens A within the metric slice, not beyond it. | **Strong within the metric-continuous slice; single-unit gap persists** |
| **B** | Context-dependent → distributed / "no privileged host" (Chen); EC hosts implicit maps, vmPFC/DMN hosts active decisions | **Re-centers EC as a genuine host and specifies an EC→mPFC division of labor.** For this *active* task the schema (grid) code is in **EC** and *mediates* inference; the mPFC distance code is **derived from** the EC grid (parallel mediation: only EC survives for map-based inference) — the reverse of the earlier "abstract grid strongest in vmPFC" reading from Constantinescu. mPFC gains an *independent* role only in **assimilation** (game 2). So not "no privileged host" but a **two-region pipeline**: EC schema → mPFC map, with a late-maturing EC–mPFC cingulum tract gating the extension to abstract space. | **EC = schema host; mPFC = derived map + independent assimilator; a pipeline, not a single locus** |
| **C** | Bet; cheapest supporting evidence (grid-presence) demoted to non-diagnostic (Chen) | **Partially re-inflated toward finding — grid-presence upgraded toward grid-*usage*.** The congruency dissociation is the key move: grid strength predicts improvement *only* on trials that provably require 2D structure (incongruent), not on heuristic-solvable ones (congruent). That is co-occurrence with a **behavioral-necessity gradient**, which is most of what Chen's usage test asked for (short of causal perturbation). Plus grid maturation tracks **fluid intelligence** (SPM/WISC), linking the code to the audit's actual target (reasoning ability), not just task accuracy. **But** the graph is still 2D-Euclidean-embeddable, orderable, and actively navigated — so C strengthens on the *metric-active* slice and the **non-embeddable symbolic slice remains a bet** (authors themselves flag graph/non-Euclidean structure as future work). | **Bet → near-finding on the metric-active slice via a behavioral-usage gradient; still a bet on the symbolic slice** |

**Net effect on the frame.** Qu is the mirror of Chen: where Chen demoted grid-*presence* to non-diagnostic, Qu supplies the missing **usage gradient** (mediation + congruency dissociation) that Chen said was required — so the abstract grid signal partially recovers its evidential weight for C, but *only* where a task isolates the structure-requiring trials. The three Axis-1 sources now triangulate a consistent refined boundary: the code is real and load-bearing for inference on **metric/orderable or transition-sampled** structure (Constantinescu continuous; Garvert discrete-implicit; Qu discrete-orderable-active + developmental + intelligence-linked), and **untested-to-absent** on genuinely non-embeddable symbolic structure. The residual open region for C is unchanged in *location* (the symbolic slice, Axis-2/3) but the frame is now better-defended everywhere short of it. The one genuinely new addition Qu makes beyond Garvert/Constantinescu is the **developmental heuristic→schema transition** — a natural experiment showing the grid code is what carries a reasoner *off* a statistical shortcut onto structured inference (see [[wiki/concepts/shortcut-reasoning.md]]), which is itself a datapoint for "the map is *used*, not incidental."

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-4 #10 (Nanda, Lee & Wattenberg 2023)

Axis 4 is the **ML-side discriminator**: does map/graph structure emerge *spontaneously* in a reasoner, or is it a metaphor the modeler imposes? OthelloGPT — an 8-layer GPT trained *only* to predict legal moves, never shown the board or rules — spontaneously builds an internal board-state world model ([[wiki/papers/emergent-linear-world-models-nanda-2023.md]]). Two moves matter for the audit: (i) the world model is **linear once probed relative to the current player** (Mine/Yours/Empty, not absolute Black/White) — the original authors called it *non-linear* only because they probed the human-obvious basis; (ii) the geometry is **causally used** — a single residual-stream vector addition flips a tile and changes predicted moves (0.10 errors vs. 2.72 null), i.e. the perturbation-based **usage test** Chen 2022 demanded, executed in silico. But the **Multiple-Circuits / MoveFirst** result shows the map is *not exclusively* used: in end-games the model computes moves via simpler non-navigational circuits before the full board is computed.

| # | Prior verdict (post-Qu) | Effect of Nanda 2023 | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong within the metric-continuous slice; single-unit gap persists | **Barely touched (different substrate), one epistemic caveat added.** Not a brain, not grid-symmetry — so it neither strengthens nor weakens A directly. Its one bearing on A is *methodological*: the same real world model looked non-linear vs. linear depending on the probe basis. This sharpens Chen's "detection is an arbitrary-threshold phenomenon" into a general warning — conclusions about *whether* a metric/linear code exists are probe-basis-dependent, so both false negatives (Li et al.'s "non-linear") and false positives are live. | **Unchanged; probe-basis-dependence caveat added to A's detection column** |
| **B** | EC = schema host; mPFC = derived map + independent assimilator (a pipeline) | **Not applicable — no biological locus.** An ANN result cannot localize the abstract host. If anything it is a *distributed*-host datapoint by analogy (the world model is smeared across layers/heads and recomputed by iterative refinement), weakly consonant with Chen's "no privileged host," but carries no anatomical weight. | **N/A (in silico); no update to B** |
| **C** | Bet → near-finding on the metric-active slice (usage gradient); still a bet on the symbolic slice | **Pushed rightward on "natural attractor" and on "causally used," with a *new* caveat that presence+usage ≠ exclusivity.** Three effects: (1) **natural-attractor** — a state-graph world model emerges from pure next-token SSL with no map imposed, direct evidence the frame is a spontaneous solution, not just a modeler's metaphor; (2) **cleanest usage test in the audit** — causal vector-arithmetic perturbation changes inference behavior, the gold-standard usage evidence Chen asked for and Qu only approximated behaviorally (here done causally, but in an artificial net); (3) **new deflationary caveat — MoveFirst** — even a genuine, causally-used world model *coexists with non-navigational shortcut circuits*, the ML mirror of `shortcut-reasoning`; map-presence-and-causal-usage does **not** license "the computation *is* navigation." Scope unchanged: Othello's board is 2D, adjacency-structured, transition-sampled — the embeddable/transition-sampled slice again — so the **non-embeddable symbolic slice remains a bet.** | **Bet → strengthened on the transition-sampled slice via a causal usage test; new "presence ≠ exclusivity" caveat; symbolic slice still a bet** |

**Net effect on the frame.** Axis 4 answers its own question in the affirmative *and* supplies the counterweight: the map is a **spontaneous, causally-used** solution (natural attractor, not imposed metaphor) — Nanda gives the single cleanest usage test in the whole audit, in silico — but the **MoveFirst / multiple-circuits** finding adds a caveat none of the biological sources surfaced: a world model can be *present and causal yet non-exclusive*, with simpler shortcut circuits doing the work whenever they suffice (exactly the `shortcut-reasoning` failure mode). So Axis 4 converts "does a map emerge?" from open to **yes (spontaneously, causally)** on the transition-sampled/embeddable slice, while independently *demoting* "grid/map present during task ⇒ reasoning is navigation" one more notch — now on ML grounds, matching Chen's neuroscience deflation from the other side. The load-bearing open region for C is unchanged: genuinely non-embeddable symbolic inference (Axis-2 program-induction / active-inference rivals remain the decisive un-ingested test). The one genuinely new addition beyond the Axis-1/3 sources is the **presence-vs-exclusivity distinction** — future usage tests must show the map is not just used but *load-bearing when a shortcut is available*, the ML analog of Qu's incongruent-trial dissociation.

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-2 #5 (Johnson, Vong, Lake & Gureckis 2021)

Johnson et al. is the audit's **first Axis-2 source** — and Axis 2 works differently from Axes 1/3/4. Those asked *"is the map present / used?"* (evidence for or against the frame's own mechanism). Axis 2 asks *"does a **rival reduction** explain the same reasoning data **without** the map?"* So the move here is not confirm/deflate the grid code — the paper contains no neural claim — but to test whether **program induction / abduction** de-centers navigation on the wiki's own target benchmark. It does, on the exact slice C was already a bet ([[wiki/papers/johnson-human-program-induction-arc-2021.md]]). N=95 humans on 40 ARC tasks (83.8% vs. 21%/57.5% Kaggle program-synthesis), with state-space, natural-language, and error analyses.

| # | Prior verdict (post-Nanda) | Effect of Johnson 2021 | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong within the metric-continuous slice; single-unit gap persists | **Untouched.** No neural/geometric measurement — behavioral cognitive science. Bears on A only obliquely: ARC's demands (discrete object individuation + open-ended rule abduction) are *not* the orderable-continuous structure A is strong on, so it neither adds nor removes grid-code evidence. | **Unchanged** |
| **B** | EC = schema host; mPFC = derived map + independent assimilator (pipeline) | **Not applicable — no biological locus.** Like Nanda, an account with no anatomical weight; cannot relocate the host. | **N/A; no update to B** |
| **C** | Bet on the symbolic slice (foldable-map caveats accumulating) | **Pushed leftward — from "the frame, weak on the symbolic slice" toward "one frame among several."** The rival reduction handles ARC *natively* with zero navigation vocabulary: (1) **abduction over an unbounded conceptual store**, not path-integration over a fixed graph — ARC concepts were built *without* a grammar, so solvers generate rules on the fly rather than navigating a pre-given hypothesis space (the direct hit on fixed-vocabulary LoT *and* on any pre-given meta-graph); (2) **flexible, task-dependent object parsing** — the objectness/individuation problem that a metric grid code does not supply; (3) **natural language as the hypothesis-generation scaffold** (description length → difficulty), a non-navigational compositional substrate. **But three things keep C a bet rather than falsified:** (i) it is an *algorithmic-level* account — silent on whether a map is present, so it cannot contradict the substrate claim, only compete with its *interpretation*; (ii) program search over a hypothesis space is *foldable* into LGD (nodes = programs/hypotheses, edges = program-space moves) — **exactly the frame-preserving absorption this audit warned about** (§ "TEM-shaped"), so the fold's availability is not evidence *for* the frame; (iii) its object-priors + compositional content is *already wiki-hosted* (Core Knowledge, shortcut-reasoning, abstract-reasoning), so it partly *complements* rather than only rivals. | **Bet → demoted toward "one contestable frame"; the rival now has a native, non-navigational account of the symbolic slice** |

**Net effect on the frame.** Axes 1/3/4 triangulated *where the map is real and used* (metric/orderable + transition-sampled/embeddable). Axis 2 attacks from the other side: it shows the **discrete-symbolic residual — the one region C never covered — has a working non-navigational owner.** Program induction explains ARC's abductive, object-parsing, language-scaffolded character *better and more parsimoniously than navigation does*, on the wiki's own benchmark. Crucially this does **not** contradict Axes 1/3/4 — the two need not compete everywhere: the honest synthesis is a **division of the reasoning space**, not a single winner. Metric/transition-sampled structure → grid/map code (Axes 1/3/4, strong); discrete non-embeddable symbolic inference → program induction / abduction (Axis 2, strong). The frame C ("*all* abstract reasoning reduces to navigation") is what breaks; the weaker reading ("navigation is the substrate for the *metric/embeddable* slice, one module beside a program-induction System 2") survives and is in fact strengthened by having its boundary drawn from the *rival's* side. The still-open decision is whether the two are genuinely distinct substrates or whether the program-induction account will itself be shown to run on an emergent map (Axis-4-style) — which only ingesting Goodman 2024 (#6) and an active-inference rival (#7) can adjudicate. **Actionable upgrade:** the [[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]] page must state explicitly that folding program induction into "navigation over a hypothesis graph" is a *modeling choice, not a finding* — LGD is a chosen lens on the symbolic slice, not the established reduction.

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-2 #6 (Goodman, Gerstenberg & Tenenbaum 2024)

Goodman et al. is the audit's **second Axis-2 source** — and the first to attack C at the **framework level** rather than the **benchmark level**. Johnson (#5) showed a rival *owns ARC*; Goodman presents a rival "one problem" reduction — the **Probabilistic Language of Thought** — that claims the *same universality LGD claims* ([[wiki/concepts/probabilistic-language-of-thought.md]], [[wiki/papers/goodman-plot-probabilistic-programs-2024.md]]). Concepts = stochastic functions in a universal PPL (Church); reasoning, learning, and theory-of-mind = **conditional inference (`query`)** over compositional generative programs; concept learning = **program induction** over a program-generating meta-program. Its decisive feature for the audit: the **universality thesis** — stochastic λ-calculus represents *any computable probability distribution* (parallel to Church–Turing) — gives PLoT's "one framework" claim **theorem-grade standing** where LGD's "reasoning ≅ navigation" is (per this audit) a *framing bet*.

| # | Prior verdict (post-Johnson) | Effect of Goodman 2024 | New verdict |
|---|---|---|---|

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-4 #12 (Tehenan, Moya, Long & Lin 2025)

Tehenan et al. is the **second Axis-4 ML-side source** — the sibling of Nanda (#10), moved from a game-specific transformer to a *general-purpose, text-only* LLM ([[wiki/papers/linear-spatial-world-models-tehenan-2025.md]]). `LLaMA-3.2-3B` builds a linear subspace ≈ isomorphic to R³ (opposite relations antipodal, independent relations orthogonal), relations **compose as vector sums** (`above + left ≈ above-left`), objects occupy consistent positions in that same frame, and the subspace is **causally steerable** (74.3% overall, strongly asymmetric). ## Re-score after ingesting Axis-1 #4 (Zheng, Hebart, … Garvert 2024)

Zheng et al. is a **reanalysis of the Garvert 2017 dataset** and the audit's first source to probe not *whether* the map is present/used but **how many maps coexist and whether they integrate**. When the same objects carry two relational structures — the implicit random-walk transition graph *and* pre-existing taxonomic-semantic similarity — the hippocampal formation forms **two parallel, anatomically separable maps** (transition → anterior EC; semantic → posterior HC; non-overlapping, each null in the other's ROI), *not* one conjunctive map ([[wiki/papers/zheng-parallel-cognitive-maps-2024.md]]). Both obey geometric-map norms; the two distances are uncorrelated; the semantic map appears **passively** (task never demanded semantics) and is **HC-formation-specific** (absent from ATL/angular gyrus). Its distinctive contributions: (i) a *lifetime-acquired, explicit, taxonomic* structure is nonetheless mapped in HC automatically — showing **embeddability, not acquisition-mode or declarative/explicit character, gates the HC map**; (ii) multiple relational graphs over one entity set are held **in parallel, selectable**, not fused.

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-3 #9 (Epstein, Patai, Julian & Spiers 2017)

Epstein et al. is the audit's **measured-review skeptic** — the complement to Chen 2022's devil's advocate ([[wiki/papers/epstein-cognitive-map-humans-2017.md]]). It is a *framing* source, not new data (its primary evidence — Constantinescu, Doeller, Tavares, Howard, Javadi — is already ingested), so it moves no evidence column directly. Its bite is structural: it decomposes cognitive-map navigation into **three separable pillars** — (i) spatial *coding* (map/grid codes), (ii) landmark *anchoring* (fixing the map's orientation/position to stable references), (iii) route *planning* — and proposes extending all three to abstract domains. The audit (and the wiki) have scored the frame almost **entirely on pillar (i)**. Two further contributions: the **context-retrieval vs. orientation** distinction (recover the right map / locate yourself on it) and the honest statement of the **topological-vs-Euclidean** coding tension.

## Re-score after ingesting Axis-2 #7 (Butz 2016) — the audit's closing source

Butz 2016 is the **third and final Axis-2 rival** and the source the Johnson/Goodman re-scores both named as still-pending "to close the audit": the **active-inference / free-energy** reduction ([[wiki/papers/butz-unified-subsymbolic-cognition-2016.md]]). Its decisive difference from the first two Axis-2 sources is the **Marr level of attack.** Johnson (#5) owned the *benchmark* (ARC); Goodman (#6) owned the *framework* title with a universality theorem — but both are computational-level and *cannot touch the navigation substrate* (the retreat position C fell to: "navigation de-privileged as *the* reduction, retained as a candidate *substrate*"). - **Hosted elsewhere?** *Confirmed* — for sub-claim B the wiki's own evidence dissolves the single-host premise (distributed operations, parallel maps, a parietal non-EC rival). **Terminal actionable upgrades (the concrete outputs of this audit):**
1. **[[wiki/concepts/latent-graph-discovery.md]]** must carry an explicit **epistemic-status caveat**: "reasoning ≅ navigation over a latent graph" is a **chosen lens**, biologically warranted (as a *substrate*) for the metric/embeddable slice, **demoted-not-falsified** by three mutually-foldable rivals; folding program-induction / PLoT / free-energy accounts *into* navigation is a **modeling choice, not a finding**; the non-embeddable symbolic slice is untested.
2. **§5.0 of the SOTA review** must be de-HC-centric: swap "the Hippocampal Formation generalizes X to reasoning" for the distributed-network / no-privileged-host framing (`mec-abstract-codes-vs-declarative-rules` already states this correctly).

## Implications

*The audit is now closed — all 12 sources ingested and the terminal re-score is filed above. The three original implications are resolved as follows.*

- **Do not tear the frame down — confirmed.** Sub-claim A survived (narrowed to the embeddable slice, scoped to the coding pillar). The fix was **precision + provenance**, not demolition: the two upgrades named at open (§5.0 de-HC-centric; explicit "chosen lens" caveat on LGD) are now the audit's terminal actionable items #1–2 above, plus two the audit *discovered* (configurator + orientation stages, item #3).
- **The metric-vs-declarative fork was load-bearing — resolved, and refined.** Axis 1 pushed the fork rightward: the System-1 entry condition is **embeddability alone** (not recency, implicitness, transition-sampling, or spatial content — Zheng/Park narrow Kumaran & Maguire to *non-embeddable declarative* graphs specifically). Axes 2 established that the **non-embeddable slice has a working non-navigational owner** (program induction / PLoT / free-energy). So the grid substrate is **a metric-embeddable-special-case module beside a non-navigational System 2**, not the central reduction.
- **Sub-claim C re-scored — demoted, not falsified.** Axis-1/3/4 evidence *strengthened* C as a **substrate on the embeddable slice** (spontaneous + causally used), but Axis-2's three rivals de-privileged it as **the reduction** (Johnson, Goodman) and as **the substrate** (Butz), and no source produced grid codes for genuinely non-embeddable structure. Terminal state: **navigation is one of ≥3 mutually-foldable lenses**; the LGD page is to be reframed as a *chosen lens*, not *the* reduction (item #1). The decisive experiment — non-embeddable symbolic code + the substrate/level-connection test — remains un-run (item #4).
