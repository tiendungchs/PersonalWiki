# Question

ARC-AGI-3 gives each game a limited action set (ACTION1–5 + CLICK(row,col)), but **what each action does differs per game** — the agent must discover action semantics, not just graph structure. Real agents face the same problem: the effect of an action is context-dependent. **Is there a neuroscience mechanism for adapting to changing action labels/vocabulary?** Training an `action_to_v` map (HICAAR block 1B) feels like a deep-learning workaround rather than a brain-inspired solution.

---

# Answer

**Yes — the mechanism is called *contextual inference*, and it is the explicit rejection of the single-context learner that `action_to_v` instantiates.**

## The core claim

Heald, Lengyel & Wolpert 2021 (*Nature* 600:489–493, the **COIN** model — COntextual INference) argue that memory **creation**, **updating**, and **expression** in sensorimotor learning are all controlled by one computation: inferring which *context* you are currently in, over a repertoire of context-specific models. The brain does **not** learn "what action A does." It maintains a **repertoire** of context-conditioned action→outcome models and infers a **posterior over which one applies now**. Adaptation is proportional to that posterior. COIN's nonparametric (Dirichlet-process) prior lets the repertoire **grow**: a new context is instantiated when no existing context explains the observations.

This is the direct answer to "the effects of our actions are not always the same": the brain never assumed they were.

**Ingested 2026-07-17** → [[wiki/papers/heald-coin-contextual-inference-2021.md]], full mechanism in [[wiki/concepts/contextual-inference.md]]. Three refinements to the framing above:

**1. Proper vs. apparent learning is the paper's actual centre of gravity** — and it sharpens this answer's thesis considerably. Behaviour changes by *either* updating memories (**proper**) *or* purely by changing how much existing memories are expressed (**apparent** — no weight change at all). Savings, anterograde interference, and environmental-consistency effects, all classically read as *learning-rate* changes, are shown to be **apparent**: in parameter-free cross-validated simulations the Kalman gain and inferred state are unchanged across conditions; only the context posterior differs. For HICAAR this means a per-game adaptation curve is **not** evidence that anything was learned — the correct null hypothesis for "the agent got better at this game" is re-weighting of a fixed repertoire, not plasticity.

**2. The creation trigger is abruptness, not magnitude** — correcting "a new context is instantiated when no existing context explains the observations" above. A *gradually* introduced perturbation creates **no** new memory however large it grows; the existing context stretches to absorb it. Creation is sensitive to the *rate* at which contradicting evidence arrives. This bears directly on the D2 follow-up question below: the accommodation trigger cannot be a pure magnitude threshold on residual error.

**3. Expression and updating read different posteriors.** Expression uses the **predicted probability** (after the cue, before acting); creation and updating use the **responsibility** (after feedback). Two separate quantities at two moments in the trial — worth preserving in any implementation, since it is what lets a cue bias which operator is expressed before any evidence from acting arrives.

## Why `action_to_v` is the wrong *type*, not the wrong *implementation*

The problem is not that `Linear(action_dim, space_dim)` is a neural net. It is the **type signature**: a function commits to **one** `v` per action, globally and permanently, learnable only by gradient descent.

- DESIGN.md already caught the symptom: *"Do not train one `action_to_v` across games — ACTION1–5 vary by game, so a shared operator averages conflicting semantics."* That is precisely COIN's critique of single-context learning: a single model **averages over contexts it should have separated**.
- But a **per-game `action_to_v` is not the fix either** — it just relocates the single-context assumption inside the game, and still needs many gradient steps to learn what a button does. The biological target is **~3 trials per cue** (Wise & Murray: 24 new mappings/day; <10% error by ~8 trials/cue) — not one press, but two orders of magnitude fewer than gradient descent, and *without* touching the weights that encode the operator space.

**The brain-inspired type is: `v` is a latent variable, not a parameter.**

> The action index is a **pointer** carrying no content. Its effect `v_a` is **inferred at play-time** from observed `(f_t, f_{t+1})` transitions, carries a posterior, and is freely re-bound per game.

## The CLICK caveat

ACTION6 is a different animal. ACTION1–5 are **pure pointers** (all content latent). `CLICK(row, col)` has **compositional** content: the coordinates carry shared cross-game structure (a click at `(r,c)` relates to position `(r,c)` in *any* game), while its *semantics* (select vs. paint vs. teleport) is latent. Click therefore needs its coordinate part in slow W and its semantic part in fast M — a **partial** binding. The `space_dim` / `action_dim` split (2026-07-15) is the seam where this can be expressed.

# Implications

1. **Do not train `action_to_v` at all — infer it.** Replace the parameter with a call to the 3A inferrer over the agent's own `(f_t, f_{t+1}, a_t)` transitions. The inferrer already exists and already returns a posterior; it is pointed at the wrong input.
2. **This wires `mem`** (STATUS item 3): the fast store's job is `action_id → v`, one-shot, reset per game. Keys are one-hot action indices — pairwise distinct and near-random, satisfying `MemoryCore`'s key semantics without the SSP-correlation worry flagged in STATUS.
3. **This collapses a STATUS risk item.** 3D's InfoGain term (item 2) subsumes action-semantics discovery: probing an ambiguous button is the highest-information-gain act available early in a game. No separate mechanism.
4. **This gives D2 (INVENT #1) a formal spec.** "When `x` stops supporting inference, something has to restructure" = COIN's CRP new-context test. Conflict-triggered accommodation is the same computation as repertoire expansion ([[wiki/concepts/memory-schemas.md]]).
5. **The multi-game corpus rung changes meaning — and carries more weight than the manifold framing implied.** It is not learning what actions do; it is learning the *repertoire of what actions can do*. **Golub 2018 (ingested 2026-07-17) upgrades this from "load-bearing" to "the whole game":** the fast regime does not sample freely from a learned manifold, it re-binds a *preserved repertoire*, and monkeys would not produce novel inside-manifold patterns even when those patterns were reachable, behaviorally optimal, and incentivized. The corpus does not need to teach a space that *contains* ARC's operators — it needs to teach the operators themselves, because play-time will not fill in the interior.
6. **Honest limit — this does not close Gap #3, and the gap is wider than "outside the manifold."** COIN/MOSAIC infer over contexts drawn from *prior experience*: they buy **vocabulary selection**, not **vocabulary invention**. Golub relocates the boundary **inward**: even *inside* the manifold, producing a pattern not already in the repertoire is the slow path (days–weeks, proposed; only a subtle hint at 1–2 h). So there are two walls, not one, and per-episode operator invention is behind the nearer of them. Contextual inference converts co-discovery into selection **only if** the corpus taught the operators nearly exhaustively — a stronger and more falsifiable bet than "taught a rich enough manifold." The hard core of Gap #3 remains open.

   **Correction (Sadtler ingested 2026-07-17):** this implication previously read "the wiki previously located the boundary at Sadtler's outside-manifold regime (days of practice)." **Sadtler measures no such thing.** The outside-manifold result is a *failure to learn within a session*; "multi-day exposure" is the authors' proposal in their discussion. Both walls are therefore *unlocated above the hours timescale* — the inner one (repertoire extension, days–weeks) is Golub's proposal, the outer one (manifold extension) is Sadtler's. Neither has an upper bound in evidence. This does not weaken the argument for the exhaustive-corpus bet — nothing has been shown to cross either wall fast — but it removes the false precision: the wiki was treating two speculative timescales as measured ones, and the honest form of implication 5 is "no experiment has demonstrated fast operator invention," not "operator invention takes days."

   **The authors make this query's distinction themselves.** Sadtler's closing speculation: **combinatorial creativity** (recombining existing cognitive elements) = generating new patterns *within* the IM; **transformational creativity** (creating new elements) = patterns *outside* it. That is the selection/invention split of Gap #3, proposed in the source's own voice and extended by its authors from motor control to cognition — the wiki's framing is not a repurposing of the motor literature against its grain.
7. **ACTION7 (undo) is not an exception to patch, it is a category error.** In COIN terms undo is not an action with a `v`; it is a **state revert**. The current exclusion is principled, not a workaround.
8. **There is a layer above `v_a` that this query had no slot for (Murray, Bussey & Wise 2000, ingested 2026-07-17).** The biology separates **strategies** from both mappings and rules, and the dissociation is sharp: hippocampectomized monkeys keep applying Repeat-Stay/Change-Shift while failing to learn any new mapping; PFv+o lesions destroy the strategy *and* the learning. A strategy exploits structure **over trials** rather than cue→action content — RS/CS cuts initial 3-choice error from 67% to 33% *before a single binding exists*. HICAAR currently has nothing at this level: 3A infers `v_a` from transitions, 3D scores information gain, but neither exploits the episode's *trial structure* (did the observation repeat? then the operator that worked is still live). **(brainstorm)** The cheapest ARC-AGI-3 analog is nearly free: on a repeated frame, re-express the last successful operator without re-inferring; on a changed frame, exclude it. That is RS/CS, and the biology says it is worth ~34 points of error before any learning happens.
9. **The rule hierarchy is an accelerator, not a prerequisite — and the source says so.** Higher-order rules "could be" bypassed entirely: each exemplar is learnable one by one without ever extracting the general rule. So an architecture lacking Gap #2's meta-graph should be predicted **slow**, not **incapable** — which is a materially different (and falsifiable) claim from the one the wiki's Badre-flavoured framing implies, and it means a flat `v_a` inferrer is a *viable-but-inefficient* baseline rather than a type error at this level.
10. **`mem`'s job is bigger than `action_id → v`.** HS holds mappings, rules, *and* strategies in the intermediate term, in parallel, pending consolidation into frontal cortical-BG modules. A fast store keyed only on action indices implements one of the three things the biological fast store carries — and the two it drops (rules, strategies) are exactly the ones that make binding fast.

# Follow-up questions

- Does `v_a` need a full posterior, or does MOSAIC-style responsibility over a **discrete** operator bank (VQ codebook, LAPA-like) suffice? Discrete makes one-shot binding trivial and connects to [[wiki/entities/vsa-model.md]]; continuous keeps 3A's calibrated uncertainty.
- What is the CRP concentration parameter's analog in D2 — i.e. what sets the threshold at which HICAAR decides "this operator is new" rather than "this is a noisy instance of a known operator"? This is the false-split/false-merge dial and likely the hardest hyperparameter in the accommodation trigger. **COIN does not answer this** — it *fixed* γ (and γᵉ) at 0.1 **by hand** to reduce free parameters, and its two fitted repertoire parameters (α, ρ) are the two that recover *worst* from synthetic data. So the state of the art on this dial is "hand-set constant," and the hardest-hyperparameter suspicion is corroborated rather than relieved. **Boettiger & D'Esposito 2005 supply the first empirical anchor**: humans given covertly structureless blocks score **below chance (35%)** — worse than the 50% free from ignoring the stimuli — by persisting in hypothesis-testing a rule that isn't there. The biological dial is set *eagerly*, and the cost is real and measurable. **(brainstorm)** This reframes the question: fitting γ to average-case regret would likely yield a *less* eager value than the brain's, so "match human binding speed" and "match human calibration" may be conflicting targets, and ARC-AGI-3 (where structure is guaranteed present by construction) is a world that rewards the eager setting.
- ~~Sadtler's outside-manifold regime took **days**.~~ **Corrected on ingest (2026-07-17): it took *at least* a session and was never shown to be learnable at all.** Is there any biological instance of *fast* repertoire extension, or is slow extension a hard constraint that ARC-AGI-3 (which demands within-episode novelty) simply violates? If the latter, the corpus must cover ARC's operator space almost exhaustively — a testable prediction. **Golub 2018 answers the narrow version and hardens the prediction**: within-manifold repertoire extension did not happen either, on 1–2 h, under standing incentive, with no evidence it was pressure-limited (more incentive did not produce more Realignment). No fast repertoire extension has been demonstrated in this paradigm at all, in or out of the manifold. The exhaustive-corpus prediction is now the *live* branch rather than the pessimistic one. **Open counter-consideration:** BCI perturbations are silent and uncued — the animal is never *told* the semantics changed. Whether repertoire extension is intrinsically slow or merely slow-without-a-pointer is exactly what the binding-rate tension below turns on, and it is the difference between "the corpus must be exhaustive" and "the corpus must be exhaustive *for uncued* changes."
- **Is the 100× binding-rate gap the presence or absence of a pointer?** Wise & Murray: ~3 trials/cue. Golub: 600–870 trials for a within-manifold re-binding. Both are billed as fast re-binding within a repertoire. The candidate reconciler (**(brainstorm)**, filed in [[wiki/empirical-tensions.md]]) is that a discrete cue supplies a *slot* to bind into and bounds the search, while the BCI animal must infer that semantics changed at all. If that holds, ARC-AGI-3's discrete ACTION1–5 are the **favourable** regime and the biological target rate for HICAAR is ~3 trials/action, not hundreds — which is the difference between a viable per-episode budget and an impossible one. This is the highest-value cheap test in this query: it is a claim about *paradigms*, testable by cueing a BCI perturbation. **Sadtler 2014 confirms the reconciler's premise is real and deliberate, not incidental:** the paradigm's uncuedness is a designed control — "we gave the monkey no indication which type of perturbation would be presented," with perturbation order randomized specifically so the animal could not anticipate it, and blinding treated as a validity requirement. So the no-pointer condition is not an artifact of BCI mechanics that a better rig would remove; it is the thing the experiment was built to enforce. That makes "is the repertoire fixed, or fixed *absent a pointer*?" a question these experiments **cannot** answer by construction — which raises the value of the cueing test from clarifying to load-bearing.
- Does the responsibility-predictor idea (predict which context applies from *cues before acting*) map onto reading a game's **visual style** as a context prior — i.e., can D1's `x` serve as MOSAIC's contextual cue input? **COIN endorses the shape of this**: its cue channel `q` is exactly "evidence available before moving," it is read by the *predicted probability* that gates expression, and cue probabilities are learned per context under their own HDP. The measured caveat is that the cue is the **weaker** of the two evidence sources (cue effect *F*=10.35 vs. perturbation *F*=21.16) — visual style should bias which operator is expressed, never override what transitions actually show.
- **Where should apparent learning live in HICAAR?** COIN's sharpest result is that most classical "learning rate" phenomena require no plasticity. HICAAR currently has no component whose job is *expression re-weighting over a fixed repertoire* — 3A returns a posterior over `v_a` but the mixture step that would turn it into behaviour is unspecified. Is apparent learning simply the mixture in block 1B, or does it need its own home? **Boettiger & D'Esposito suggest the split is architectural, not a mode of one module**: acquiring and executing the same rule type recruit disjoint circuits under matched stimuli and actions, and only the inference side (dlPFC/SMA) decays as the rule is acquired — the executor and valuator (premotor, striatum) do not. **(brainstorm)** That argues for expression having its own home rather than being a flag on 3A, and it hands the wiki a candidate *discriminator* for the proper/apparent null that behaviour alone cannot provide: selective disengagement of the inferrer while the executor persists is what "something was written" looks like from outside the adaptation curve. Instrumenting 3A's posterior entropy against 1B's activity over a game would be the cheap test. **Golub 2018 supplies the population-level picture and a third category**: Reassociation is repertoire-preserved but binding-*rewritten* — neither proper learning (the repertoire/states are untouched) nor apparent learning (the intent→pattern map genuinely changed). So the home for expression re-weighting should be a **binding table over a frozen operator bank**, which is exactly `mem`'s type signature (implication 2) — the answer to "does apparent learning need its own home" is that `mem` already *is* that home, and the question was really where the *bank* lives. Golub puts the bank in the executor's inputs, not its weights.
- **Does the operator bank need to be a separate module from the inferrer?** The 63%-of-variance result is about *coupling*: what predicted human learning was neither dlPFC nor SMA activity but their correlation. If SMA/pre-SMA is the candidate-mapping bank and dlPFC the thing that biases selection within it, then HICAAR's bottleneck is the 3A↔1B interface rather than either block's capacity — a claim no current STATUS item measures.
- **Does dlPFC's categorical-rules-only recruitment mean action semantics and node abstraction are the same problem?** dlPFC showed up here and not in a decade of prior 1:1-mapping imaging because the rule grouped 10 stimuli under one latent constellation → one button. That is simultaneously an action-semantics problem (which button) and a node-content abstraction problem (which stimuli are the same). If the frontal hierarchy engages *in proportion to how much lower-order structure the rule compresses* ([[wiki/papers/frontal-cortex-abstract-rules-badre2010.md]]), then inferring `v_a` and discovering the state abstraction may not be separable stages — which would cut against 3A and 1B being cleanly ordered.
- **Does COIN's WM localization constrain block 3C?** Heald et al. map working memory onto maintenance of *context responsibilities* — a WM task abolishes spontaneous recovery by resetting predicted probabilities to stationary values. If WM's job is "which model am I currently using," that is a much narrower and more implementable spec than "goal maintenance," and it is testable against the PFC hierarchy account in [[wiki/concepts/cognitive-control.md]]. **Murray, Bussey & Wise 2000 clear the ground for this from the lesion side**: they explicitly reject storage as PF's function in this task — the instruction stimulus stayed **on-screen through choice and execution** (near-zero storage demand) yet the PFv+o deficit was profound, and PFv-lesioned monkeys hold stimuli for 8s fine (Rushworth 1997). Their conclusion — this "falsifies the widely promoted hypothesis that the sole specialized function of PF, as a whole, is the mediation of working memory" — makes COIN's narrow spec *more* attractive, not less: if PF is not a buffer, "which model am I using" is a live candidate for what its delay activity is actually for. **(brainstorm)** The two accounts fit: a responsibility posterior is not *storage* in the sense the lesion work rules out — it is a quantity that must be maintained while a mapping is inferred, which is exactly the profile of activity that decays once the mapping is acquired (Boettiger). The wiki should stop treating "PFC delay activity" as prima facie evidence of a buffer.
