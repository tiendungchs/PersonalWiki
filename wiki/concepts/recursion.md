# Recursion / Discrete Infinity

**Recursion = a computational operation that embeds its own output as input, taking a finite set of elements and yielding a potentially infinite array of discrete expressions ("discrete infinity"). Hauser, Chomsky & Fitch (2002) hypothesize this is the single uniquely-human computational core of language (FLN).**

Discrete infinity is the property that a system has no longest expression (any candidate can be embedded in a larger one — "Mary thinks that …") and no non-integer intermediate (6-word and 7-word sentences, no 6.5-word). It is *directly analogous to the natural numbers* — both are generated by an unbounded successor-style operation over discrete units.

---

## FLB vs. FLN (the paper's central cut)

| | Faculty of Language — Broad (FLB) | Faculty of Language — Narrow (FLN) |
|---|---|---|
| Contents | sensory-motor system + conceptual-intentional system + recursion | recursion (narrow syntax) alone + its interface mappings |
| Evolutionary status | ancient, largely **shared** with other vertebrates (continuity is the null hypothesis) | recently evolved, **uniquely human** (HCF Hypothesis 3) |
| ML reading | the perceptual/semantic modules a model already has | the missing combinatorial engine that yields productivity |

The strong claim: most of the apparent complexity of language lives in the *peripheral* FLB modules (shared, quantitative differences); what is qualitatively new is only the recursive operator and its mappings to the interfaces.

---

## The Chomsky Hierarchy (the formal tool)

Grammars form a strict complexity hierarchy; the load-bearing boundary for reasoning is regular vs. context-free:

| Class | Dependency type | Can capture natural language? | Animal evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Finite-state / regular** | local, adjacent only; transitional probabilities | No | tamarins, infants, across modalities — **shared** |
| **Phrase-structure / context-free** | long-distance, hierarchical, recursively embedded | Minimum required | tamarins **fail**; humans succeed |
| context-sensitive ⊂ recursively enumerable | — | superset | — |

**AₙBₙ vs. ABₙ (Fitch & Hauser 2004):** the cleanest probe. `ABₙ` (any number of AB pairs) is finite-state and cotton-top tamarins learn it; `AₙBₙ` (n A's then n B's — requires counting/center-embedding, i.e. a stack) is context-free and tamarins fail it in three experiments, while human adults acquire it implicitly. Statistical/transitional-probability learning (Saffran-style word segmentation) sits at the *finite-state* floor and is spontaneously available to infants, tamarins, and across vision/audition — so it is **not** the uniquely-human ingredient.

## The Domain-General Recursion Hypothesis

HCF's most generative speculation for this wiki: FLN may have **evolved for reasons other than communication** — number, navigation, or social relations — and comparative work should therefore hunt for recursion *outside* the communicative domain.

- **(brainstorm)** A candidate evolutionary trajectory: recursion begins as a **modular, domain-specific** operator (e.g. dedicated to path integration / foraging search), then becomes **penetrable and domain-general**, letting a single recursive engine range over numbers, words, and plans. This "modular → domain-general" transition is the same move the wiki's cross-module "common code" hypothesis names (see [[wiki/concepts/compositional-generalization.md]]).
- This directly parallels the wiki's core claim that the same grid/graph machinery serving navigation is reused for abstract relational reasoning ([[wiki/concepts/successor-representation.md]], [[wiki/concepts/structural-generalization.md]]).

## Why This Matters for a Reasoning Model

1. **Recursion = the productivity facet.** Discrete infinity is exactly the "generalize to sequences longer than training" property where transformers score ~0.50 and MLC hits 100% error on SCAN-length ([[wiki/concepts/compositional-generalization.md]]). HCF names it as the *defining* capability and localizes it to a single operator — sharpening the target from "be more compositional" to "acquire a recursive/stack-like combinator."
2. **The meta-graph must be recursive.** A flat meta-graph W cannot represent rules-embedded-in-rules; recursion is the formal content of [[wiki/architectural-gaps.md]] Gap #2 (multi-level meta-graph). Phrase-structure ⊃ finite-state is the grammar-theoretic statement of why a nested/hierarchical W is required and a Markov/finite-state transition model is provably insufficient.
3. **Shared substrate vs. unique combinator.** FLB/FLN maps cleanly onto the wiki's recurring split: perceptual/world-model modules are largely reusable (shared); the missing piece is the small combinatorial operator that composes them productively — not a new perceptual prior.
4. **Discrete infinity ≅ natural numbers.** The successor function unifies recursion with the [[wiki/concepts/core-knowledge.md]] number system; a model that acquires the integer successor rule has, in miniature, the same generative operator language needs.

## Open Problems

1. **What is the minimal neural implementation of a stack/center-embedding?** Finite-state dynamics are generic to RNNs; a bounded push-down memory is not — which wiki mechanism (WM gating, replay, external memory) supplies it?
2. **Is recursion learned or an innate operator?** HCF leave it open whether FLN is an adaptation or a by-product ("spandrel"); the ML analog is whether productivity must be architecturally built in or can emerge from a sufficiently structured meta-training distribution ([[wiki/entities/mlc-model.md]] suggests the latter is bounded).
3. **Does animal recursion exist in a non-communicative domain?** If foraging/navigation search is genuinely recursive, the operator predates language and the engineering target is *penetrability* (domain-general routing), not the operator itself.
